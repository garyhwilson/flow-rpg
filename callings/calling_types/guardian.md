# The Guardian
*"I exist to help others"*

## Core Identity
- **Core Drive:** To feel needed and appreciated through service
- **Fear:** Being unloved or unwanted
- **Desire:** To feel loved and needed by others

## Mechanical Benefits
- **Flow Benefit - Protective Presence:** When an ally within Close range takes damage, you gain +1 Flow (once per round maximum)
- **Narrative Permission - Helper's Intuition:** You always know when someone genuinely needs help, even if they're trying to hide it. No roll needed

## Moral Compass
Guardians base decisions on how they affect others, particularly those they care about. They see morality through the lens of relationships and community needs, often sacrificing for others' benefit.

## In Action
- Puts others' needs before their own
- Can be selfless heroes or manipulative martyrs
- Draws strength from being needed
- May neglect self-care or become possessive

## Background Hooks
- Healer who couldn't save someone important
- Protector of a destroyed community
- Parent figure to orphans or outcasts
- Former servant seeking to help on their own terms
- Cleric devoted to healing deity
- Bodyguard who failed their charge

## Playing a Guardian
Your connections to others define your heroism. Build relationships with party members and NPCs. Your growth comes from learning that true help sometimes means letting others stand on their own.

## Tactical Approach
### Guardian in Combat
- Gain Flow when allies are hurt
- Spend Flow on healing and protection
- Position to shield vulnerable allies
- Use Rally action frequently

### Stance Synergies
- **Aggressive:** Rarely used, except to quickly reach endangered allies
- **Balanced:** Used when needing flexibility to respond to multiple allies' needs
- **Defensive:** Preferred stance, allowing maximum protection for others

## Example in Play
*Marcus the Guardian sees the party rogue drop to 5/12 Vitality from an orc's brutal attack. His Protective Surge triggers immediately, granting him 1 Flow. Earlier in town, he noticed a seemingly cheerful merchant was actually desperate for aid - no roll needed, he just knew.*

## Calling Evolution
- **Growth:** Learning healthy boundaries and self-care
- **Stress:** Becoming controlling or codependent
- **Integration:** Helping others help themselves

## Relationships with Other Callings

### Natural Allies
- **Champion:** Both protective, with Guardian focusing on care and Champion on strength
- **Mediator:** Guardian's concern for others pairs well with Mediator's desire for harmony
- **Advocate:** Guardian protects individuals while Advocate fights for justice

### Potential Tensions
- **Seeker:** May view the Seeker's self-focus as selfish
- **Exemplar:** May disagree on prioritizing achievement over helping others
- **Enthusiast:** Sometimes frustrated by the Enthusiast's freedom-seeking

## Archetype Synergies

### Strong Combinations
- **Guardian Cleric:** Channels divine compassion to mend wounds and shelter the vulnerable
- **Guardian Fighter:** Stands firm between danger and those they've sworn to protect
- **Guardian Paladin:** Sacred oaths made manifest through protective devotion

### Challenging Combinations
- **Guardian Rogue:** Reconciling helping others with self-reliance
- **Guardian Wizard:** Balancing intellectual pursuits with emotional connection

## Species Considerations
- **Human Guardians:** Often focused on community building and institutional care
- **Dwarf Guardians:** Typically protective of clan traditions and ancestral legacy
- **Elf Guardians:** May adopt mentorship roles spanning generations
- **Halfling Guardians:** Usually centered on hearth, home, and community well-being

## Tactical Positioning
Guardians excel at defensive positioning:

- **Zone Positioning:** Always positions between allies and threats
- **Movement Priority:** Reaching endangered allies quickly
- **Combat Role:** Drawing attacks away from vulnerable party members
- **Resource Management:** Spends Flow primarily on healing and protection

## Equipment Preferences
- **Weapons:** Often chooses defensive weapons (shields, reach weapons)
- **Armor:** Prioritizes protection over mobility
- **Items:** Carries healing supplies, bandages, and protective talismans
- **Magic:** Focuses on restorative and protective spells

## Connections to Flow
The Guardian's relationship with Flow is deeply tied to their protective nature:

- **Flow Generation:** Primarily through protecting others and responding to allies in danger
- **Flow Expenditure:** Mainly on defensive techniques and healing abilities
- **Flow States:** Often maintains moderate positive Flow to be ready for emergencies
- **Flow Philosophy:** Views Flow as a resource for others' benefit rather than personal gain
