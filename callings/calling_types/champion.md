# The Champion

_"Strength protects what matters"_

## Core Identity

- **Core Drive:** To be self-reliant, in control, and protect others
- **Fear:** Being controlled, vulnerable, or weak
- **Desire:** To be self-reliant and in control of their destiny

## Mechanical Benefits

- **Momentum Benefit - Protective Wrath:** While any ally is below half Guard or Vitality, you gain +1 Momentum on all successful attacks
- **Narrative Permission - Unshakeable Will:** Intimidation attempts against you automatically fail. You project strength that cannot be undermined

## Moral Compass

Champions see morality through the lens of power and justice. They believe might should protect right, and that the strong have a duty to defend those who cannot defend themselves.

## In Action

- Takes charge and protects others
- Can be heroic defenders or domineering tyrants
- Respects strength while defending the vulnerable
- May become controlling or confrontational

## Background Hooks

- Tribal leader protecting their people
- Former oppressor learning to use power justly
- Street fighter defending the helpless
- Overthrown ruler reclaiming their purpose
- Freed slave becoming liberator
- Monster hunter protecting villages

## Playing a Champion

Your strength gives the party confidence and protection. Lead from the front while respecting others' autonomy. Growth comes from discovering that true strength includes vulnerability and interdependence.

## Tactical Approach

### Champion in Combat

- Generate extra Momentum when protecting in Aggressive Stance
- Spend Momentum on devastating attacks
- Cannot be intimidated or frightened
- Position between allies and threats

### Stance Synergies

- **Aggressive:** Preferred stance, maximizing protective offensive capability
- **Balanced:** Used when tactical versatility is needed
- **Defensive:** Adopted when protection requires absorbing damage

## Example in Play

_Kragen the Champion stands between the dragon and fallen allies. His attack succeeds while protecting others, generating an additional Momentum point from Protective Dominance. When the dragon tries to frighten him with its terrible presence, Kragen's Unshakeable Will means he doesn't even need to roll - he simply stares back unfazed._

## Calling Evolution

- **Growth:** Strength includes gentleness and vulnerability
- **Stress:** Becoming tyrannical or vengeful
- **Integration:** Power serves love, not control

## Relationships with Other Callings

### Natural Allies

- **Guardian:** Complementary protective approaches
- **Exemplar:** Shared appreciation for strength and capability
- **Advocate:** Champion's strength can enforce Advocate's justice

### Potential Tensions

- **Mediator:** May view compromise as weakness
- **Seeker:** Can clash over autonomy versus protection
- **Scholar:** Different approaches to problem-solving (force vs. analysis)

## Archetype Synergies

### Strong Combinations

- **Champion Fighter:** The quintessential warrior protecting through martial prowess
- **Champion Paladin:** Divine authority reinforcing protective strength
- **Champion Barbarian:** Primal power channeled toward defense of others

### Challenging Combinations

- **Champion Wizard:** Balancing physical dominance with arcane study
- **Champion Bard:** Reconciling forceful protection with inspirational leadership

## Species Considerations

- **Human Champions:** Often driven to establish legacy through protective achievements
- **Dwarf Champions:** Typically defend clan honor and ancestral holdings
- **Elf Champions:** May protect traditions and knowledge across centuries
- **Halfling Champions:** Frequently underestimated protectors of home and community

## Power Dynamics

How Champions approach strength and authority:

- **Controlled Strength:** Mastering power rather than being mastered by it
- **Authority Establishment:** Creating clear leadership hierarchies
- **Decisive Action:** Taking charge when others hesitate
- **Boundary Setting:** Establishing and enforcing limits
- **Challenge Response:** Meeting threats with appropriate force

## Protection Philosophy

The Champion's approach to defending others:

- **Preemptive Defense:** Neutralizing threats before they cause harm
- **Protective Positioning:** Placing themselves between danger and those protected
- **Demonstration of Strength:** Deterring aggression through visible capability
- **Selective Vulnerability:** Choosing when to risk personal safety
- **Empowerment Teaching:** Helping others develop their own strength

## Leadership Styles

Champions typically lead in one of several ways:

- **Command Leadership:** Direct, clear instructions and expectations
- **Example Setting:** Demonstrating desired behaviors through personal action
- **Strength Recognition:** Identifying and developing capabilities in others
- **Challenge Providing:** Creating opportunities for growth through testing
- **Protective Guidance:** Leading to ensure others' safety and success

## The Champion's Momentum

The Champion's relationship with Momentum reflects their protective strength:

- **Momentum Generation:** Through successful protection of others while in Aggressive Stance
- **Momentum Expenditure:** On powerful, decisive techniques that demonstrate strength
- **Momentum States:** Thrives in high-Momentum situations showcasing protective capability
- **Momentum Philosophy:** Views Momentum as the power to protect what matters

## Growth Challenges

The Champion's journey involves several key developments:

- **Controlled Power:** Mastering strength without becoming domineering
- **Vulnerability Acceptance:** Finding strength in openness and interdependence
- **Protection Balance:** Defending others without controlling them
- **Integrated Strength:** Combining physical power with emotional and spiritual strength
