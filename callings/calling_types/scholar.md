# The Scholar

_"Knowledge reveals truth"_

## Core Identity

- **Core Drive:** To understand, comprehend, and master knowledge
- **Fear:** Being incompetent, invaded, or overwhelmed
- **Desire:** To be capable and understanding

## Mechanical Benefits

- **Momentum Benefit - Perpetual Readiness:** You begin each scene with +1 Momentum instead of 0, representing your perpetual state of mental preparedness
- **Narrative Permission - Contingency Planning:** Once per scene, you can declare you prepared for this exact situation and produce a relevant tool, fact, or minor preparation

## Moral Compass

Scholars base ethics on logic, knowledge, and understanding. They believe right action comes from right thinking, often taking time to analyze before making moral judgments.

## In Action

- Observes and analyzes before acting
- Can be wise advisors or detached observers
- Values privacy and intellectual independence
- May become isolated or overly theoretical

## Background Hooks

- Sage whose theories were catastrophically wrong
- Investigator of forbidden knowledge
- Former spy or information broker
- Librarian protecting dangerous secrets
- Wizard seeking ultimate spell
- Detective solving impossible case

## Playing a Scholar

Your knowledge provides crucial insights for the party. Share your expertise while remaining engaged with the world. Growth comes from balancing analysis with action and connecting your heart to your formidable mind.

## Tactical Approach

### Scholar in Combat

- Bank Momentum between encounters
- Spend Momentum on tactical advantages
- Identify enemy weaknesses
- Always have the right tool ready

### Stance Synergies

- **Aggressive:** Rarely used, except when analytical conclusion demands immediate action
- **Balanced:** Preferred stance, allowing observation and calculated response
- **Defensive:** Used for careful analysis of complex threats

## Example in Play

_Otto the Scholar successfully identifies the demon's weakness through research, gaining 1 Momentum. Instead of using it immediately, he banks it (he can save up to 2). Later, when ambushed, he declares: "Good thing I packed silver dust, expecting werewolves on this road."_

## Calling Evolution

- **Growth:** Connecting knowledge to emotional wisdom
- **Stress:** Retreating into pure intellectualism
- **Integration:** Wisdom includes feeling and doing

## Relationships with Other Callings

### Natural Allies

- **Advocate:** Both seek truth, though for different purposes
- **Sentinel:** Share concern with preparation and foresight
- **Mediator:** Can provide factual foundation for harmonious solutions

### Potential Tensions

- **Enthusiast:** May view the Enthusiast as impulsive or shallow
- **Champion:** Can clash over analysis versus action
- **Seeker:** Different approaches to truth-seeking (external vs. internal)

## Archetype Synergies

### Strong Combinations

- **Scholar Wizard:** The arcane researcher pursuing magical knowledge
- **Scholar Cleric:** The theologian seeking divine understanding
- **Scholar Rogue:** The investigator solving mysteries through careful observation

### Challenging Combinations

- **Scholar Fighter:** Balancing theoretical knowledge with physical application
- **Scholar Bard:** Reconciling analytical thinking with emotional expression

## Species Considerations

- **Human Scholars:** Often driven to efficient learning given their limited lifespan
- **Dwarf Scholars:** Typically methodical, building knowledge over generations
- **Elf Scholars:** May pursue multiple fields of study across centuries
- **Halfling Scholars:** Frequently focus on practical knowledge with immediate applications

## Knowledge Domains

Scholars typically specialize in specific areas of expertise:

- **Academic Fields:** Specialized subjects like history, alchemy, or theology
- **Practical Disciplines:** Applied knowledge like medicine, engineering, or navigation
- **Esoteric Studies:** Rare knowledge like ancient languages, forgotten rituals, or planar theory
- **Analytical Methods:** Investigative techniques, logical systems, or research methodologies

## Research Methods

How Scholars pursue and organize knowledge:

- **Field Research:** Direct observation and data collection
- **Comparative Analysis:** Identifying patterns across different sources
- **Experimental Testing:** Verifying theories through controlled trials
- **Collaborative Exchange:** Sharing findings with other knowledge-seekers
- **Archival Study:** Examining historical records and preserved information

## Knowledge Ethics

Scholars face unique ethical questions:

- **Knowledge Stewardship:** When to share versus safeguard dangerous information
- **Research Boundaries:** Ethical limits on methods of knowledge acquisition
- **Truth Obligations:** Responsibility to correct misinformation
- **Application Responsibility:** Accountability for how knowledge is used

## The Scholar's Momentum

The Scholar's relationship with Momentum reflects their analytical nature:

- **Momentum Generation:** Through successful application of knowledge and analysis
- **Momentum Banking:** Unique ability to preserve Momentum between scenes
- **Momentum Expenditure:** On calculated, precision techniques
- **Momentum Philosophy:** Views Momentum as a resource to be managed efficiently

## Intellectual Growth

The Scholar's developmental journey:

- **Specialized Knowledge:** Deep expertise in chosen fields
- **Interdisciplinary Connections:** Recognizing patterns across different domains
- **Applied Wisdom:** Moving from theoretical understanding to practical application
- **Integrated Knowledge:** Connecting intellectual understanding with emotional and physical wisdom
