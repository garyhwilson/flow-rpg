# The Seeker
*"I must find my true self"*

## Core Identity
- **Core Drive:** To understand themselves and find their unique identity
- **Fear:** Having no identity or significance
- **Desire:** To find themselves and their significance

## Mechanical Benefits
- **Flow Benefit - Authentic Action:** When you act against conventional wisdom or expectations in a way true to yourself, gain 1 Flow (once per scene)
- **Narrative Permission - Soul Reading:** Once per scene, you can ask one creature a deep personal question that they must answer honestly (though they may be cryptic)

## Moral Compass
Seekers make decisions based on authenticity and personal meaning. They reject conventional morality in favor of individual truth, seeing ethics as deeply personal rather than universal.

## In Action
- Pursues unique paths and unconventional solutions
- Can be visionary artists or self-absorbed outcasts
- Values emotional truth over practical concerns
- May become moody or envious of others

## Background Hooks
- Artist searching for the perfect masterpiece
- Cursed individual seeking their true nature
- Outsider who never fit their birth culture
- Memory-lost wanderer discovering their past
- Shapeshifter exploring identity
- Noble rejecting inherited role

## Playing a Seeker
Your quest for identity drives fascinating character development. Embrace your uniqueness while connecting with others who appreciate your authentic self. Growth comes from realizing you can be unique while still belonging.

## Tactical Approach
### Seeker in Combat
- Gain Flow for unconventional tactics
- Spend Flow on creative solutions
- Use environment in unexpected ways
- Fight with personalized style

### Stance Synergies
- **Aggressive:** Used when authentic self-expression demands bold action
- **Balanced:** Common choice, allowing flexibility for authentic response
- **Defensive:** Used when self-preservation aligns with personal truth

## Example in Play
*Lyrian the Seeker refuses to use the "proper" magical components, instead channeling power through their self-made crystal focus. This authentic approach triggers 1 Flow gain. Later, they look the villain in the eye and ask, "What did you sacrifice to become this?" The villain must answer, though cryptically: "Everything that made her love me."*

## Calling Evolution
- **Growth:** Finding identity includes connection to others
- **Stress:** Becoming isolated or melancholic
- **Integration:** Authentic self includes relationships

## Relationships with Other Callings

### Natural Allies
- **Enthusiast:** Both value personal freedom and authentic experience
- **Scholar:** Both pursue understanding, though with different focuses
- **Mediator:** Can appreciate the Seeker's unique perspective

### Potential Tensions
- **Exemplar:** Clash between internal versus external validation
- **Sentinel:** May find the Sentinel's caution restrictive
- **Guardian:** May view the Guardian's focus on others as neglecting self-discovery

## Archetype Synergies

### Strong Combinations
- **Seeker Wizard:** The magical researcher exploring personal arcane expression
- **Seeker Bard:** The artist whose performances reveal deeper truths about self
- **Seeker Ranger:** The wanderer finding identity through connection with nature

### Challenging Combinations
- **Seeker Paladin:** Reconciling personal truth with oath-bound service
- **Seeker Fighter:** Finding authenticity within martial discipline

## Species Considerations
- **Human Seekers:** Often driven by urgency to discover self within limited lifespan
- **Dwarf Seekers:** May struggle between ancestral traditions and personal identity
- **Elf Seekers:** Can explore multiple identities and paths over centuries
- **Halfling Seekers:** Frequently navigate tension between community connection and individuality

## Identity Exploration
Seekers employ various methods to discover themselves:

- **Creative Expression:** Art, music, writing as windows to the authentic self
- **Cultural Experimentation:** Trying different traditions and lifestyles
- **Relationship Testing:** Understanding self through connections with others
- **Physical Challenges:** Pushing boundaries to reveal true capabilities
- **Spiritual Practices:** Meditation, vision quests, or other introspective techniques

## Authenticity Markers
How Seekers recognize and express their true selves:

- **Emotional Resonance:** Feeling "right" about choices and expressions
- **Personal Symbolism:** Creating or adopting symbols that represent inner truth
- **Consistent Core:** Maintaining certain values despite external changes
- **Rejection of Pretense:** Refusing to conform to expectations that feel false

## The Question of Belonging
A central tension for most Seekers:

- **Uniqueness vs. Connection:** Balancing distinct identity with meaningful relationships
- **Community Boundaries:** Finding groups that accept authentic self-expression
- **Conditional Acceptance:** Struggling with environments that demand conformity
- **Created Family:** Building relationships with those who appreciate their true nature

## The Seeker's Flow
The Seeker's relationship with Flow is deeply personal:

- **Flow Generation:** Through authentic self-expression and unconventional approaches
- **Flow Expenditure:** On actions that honor their unique perspective
- **Flow States:** Most comfortable when Flow arises from true self rather than external expectations
- **Flow Philosophy:** Views Flow as affirmation of personal truth
