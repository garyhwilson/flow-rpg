# The Sentinel

_"Loyalty and vigilance keep us safe"_

## Core Identity

- **Core Drive:** To find security and support through loyalty and preparation
- **Fear:** Being without support, guidance, or security
- **Desire:** To have security and support

## Mechanical Benefits

- **Momentum Benefit - Ever Watchful:** Whenever you or an ally you can see is attacked, gain +1 Momentum (once per round maximum)
- **Narrative Permission - Danger Sense:** You cannot be surprised or ambushed. You always get to act in the first round of combat, even if caught off-guard

## Moral Compass

Sentinels base decisions on loyalty, duty, and protecting their community. They see morality through the lens of responsibility and preparedness, often questioning but ultimately committing deeply.

## In Action

- Questions and tests before trusting
- Can be steadfast allies or anxious doubters
- Prepares for problems before they arise
- May become paranoid or overly dependent

## Background Hooks

- Betrayed soldier seeking trustworthy companions
- Town guard who failed to prevent disaster
- Loyal retainer whose master died
- Scout who must always watch for danger
- Spy struggling with conflicting loyalties
- Knight questioning corrupt orders

## Playing a Sentinel

Your loyalty makes you the party's backbone. Build trust slowly but deeply. Your growth comes from learning when to trust your own judgment over external authorities or excessive doubt.

## Tactical Approach

### Sentinel in Combat

- Gain defensive bonuses with Momentum
- Spend Momentum on protective actions
- Never caught off-guard
- Excel at watch duties and ambush detection

### Stance Synergies

- **Aggressive:** Rarely used, except to quickly address identified threats
- **Balanced:** Used when monitoring multiple potential dangers
- **Defensive:** Preferred stance, maximizing vigilance and protection

## Example in Play

_Gareth the Sentinel strikes down a goblin, gaining 1 Momentum. His Defensive Vigilance also grants him +1 Guard until his next turn. Later, when cultists spring an ambush, the GM notes that while others might be surprised, Gareth's Danger Sense means he acts normally in round one._

## Calling Evolution

- **Growth:** Trusting inner authority over external
- **Stress:** Becoming paranoid or blindly loyal
- **Integration:** Balanced skepticism with faith

## Relationships with Other Callings

### Natural Allies

- **Guardian:** Both protective, with complementary approaches
- **Advocate:** Sentinel provides security while Advocate ensures justice
- **Champion:** Share protective instincts, though with different methods

### Potential Tensions

- **Enthusiast:** May view the Enthusiast as recklessly impulsive
- **Seeker:** Can clash over security versus exploration
- **Exemplar:** Different priorities regarding safety versus achievement

## Archetype Synergies

### Strong Combinations

- **Sentinel Fighter:** The vigilant protector always ready for threats
- **Sentinel Ranger:** The scout who detects danger before it strikes
- **Sentinel Rogue:** The security specialist who understands all vulnerabilities

### Challenging Combinations

- **Sentinel Bard:** Balancing vigilance with performance and inspiration
- **Sentinel Wizard:** Reconciling practical security with arcane exploration

## Species Considerations

- **Human Sentinels:** Often focused on institutional security and social stability
- **Dwarf Sentinels:** Typically guardians of ancestral traditions and clan security
- **Elf Sentinels:** May watch over territories or knowledge across centuries
- **Halfling Sentinels:** Frequently protect community comfort and way of life

## Security Protocols

How Sentinels maintain vigilance:

- **Perimeter Security:** Establishing safe boundaries and monitoring approaches
- **Threat Assessment:** Evaluating potential dangers and prioritizing responses
- **Contingency Planning:** Preparing for various scenarios before they occur
- **Information Security:** Protecting sensitive knowledge from unauthorized access
- **Trust Verification:** Testing loyalty and reliability of allies

## Loyalty Dynamics

The complex nature of Sentinel allegiance:

- **Loyalty Hierarchy:** Prioritizing commitments when obligations conflict
- **Trust Building:** The gradual process of extending confidence to others
- **Authority Relationship:** Navigating tension between loyalty and questioning
- **Commitment Testing:** How Sentinels verify the worthiness of their allegiance

## Vigilance Patterns

Distinctive awareness strategies:

- **Environmental Scanning:** Continuous monitoring for changes and anomalies
- **Pattern Recognition:** Identifying deviations that signal potential threats
- **Risk Mitigation:** Proactively addressing vulnerabilities
- **Alert States:** Different levels of readiness based on perceived danger
- **Recovery Planning:** Preparation for what happens after security fails

## The Sentinel's Momentum

The Sentinel's relationship with Momentum reflects their protective nature:

- **Momentum Generation:** Through successful vigilance and threat neutralization
- **Momentum Enhancement:** Unique ability to convert Momentum into defensive Guard
- **Momentum Expenditure:** On awareness and protective techniques
- **Momentum Philosophy:** Views Momentum as a resource for maintaining security

## Growth Challenges

The Sentinel's journey involves several key developments:

- **Trust Calibration:** Finding balance between skepticism and faith
- **Inner Authority:** Developing confidence in personal judgment
- **Flexibility:** Adapting security approaches to changing circumstances
- **Proportional Response:** Matching vigilance to actual danger
