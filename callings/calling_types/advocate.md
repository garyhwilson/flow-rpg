# The Advocate

_"The world must be made right"_

## Core Identity

- **Core Drive:** To improve, perfect, and bring order to chaos
- **Fear:** Being corrupt, defective, or wrong
- **Desire:** To be good, right, accurate, and virtuous

## Mechanical Benefits

- **Momentum Benefit - Truth Revealed:** When you or an ally succeeds at revealing truth or exposing deception (any Investigation, Empathy, or social check that uncovers hidden information), gain +1 Momentum
- **Narrative Permission - Truth Sense:** You can always tell when someone is lying about wrongdoing or corruption. No roll needed - you simply know

## Moral Compass

Advocates make decisions based on their internal sense of right and wrong. They hold themselves and others to high standards, seeing potential for improvement everywhere. Their ethics are typically rigid but deeply personal.

## In Action

- Seeks justice and fairness in all situations
- Can be inspiring reformers or inflexible judges
- Often struggles between ideals and practical reality
- May become self-righteous or overly critical

## Background Hooks

- Former judge or lawkeeper seeking true justice
- Fallen noble trying to restore honor
- Revolutionary fighting systematic corruption
- Perfectionist craftsperson or artist
- Paladin who left corrupt church
- Investigator exposing conspiracies

## Playing an Advocate

Your sense of right and wrong drives every decision. Use your high standards to inspire others, but beware of becoming inflexible. Your greatest growth comes from accepting imperfection while maintaining your ideals.

## Tactical Approach

### Advocate in Combat

- Gain Momentum when fighting corrupt enemies
- Spend Momentum to expose enemy weaknesses
- Rally allies with righteous speeches
- Target leaders of evil organizations

### Stance Synergies

- **Aggressive:** Use when directly confronting injustice, channeling righteous anger
- **Balanced:** Preferred stance for gathering evidence and making fair judgments
- **Defensive:** Use when protecting victims of corruption or preserving crucial evidence

## Example in Play

_Sera the Advocate confronts a corrupt merchant. As she reveals his theft from the orphanage, her Righteous Fury triggers - she gains 1 Momentum. Earlier, when the merchant tried to lie about his "charitable donations," Sera's player simply stated "I know he's lying" - no roll needed._

## Calling Evolution

- **Growth:** Learning when mercy serves justice better than punishment
- **Stress:** Becoming increasingly rigid and judgmental
- **Integration:** Balancing high standards with compassion

## Relationships with Other Callings

### Natural Allies

- **Scholar:** Truth-seeking combines well with the Advocate's pursuit of justice
- **Champion:** Strength to enforce justice complements the Advocate's moral clarity
- **Sentinel:** Both value protection against wrongdoing, though with different approaches

### Potential Tensions

- **Mediator:** May clash over prioritizing peace versus justice
- **Enthusiast:** The Advocate may view the Enthusiast as lacking moral seriousness
- **Exemplar:** May disagree on whether ends justify means

## Archetype Synergies

### Strong Combinations

- **Advocate Paladin:** The classic champion of justice, combining moral conviction with divine authority
- **Advocate Wizard:** Uses arcane knowledge to uncover and combat corruption
- **Advocate Rogue:** The vigilante who works outside the system to deliver justice

### Challenging Combinations

- **Advocate Bard:** Must balance entertainment with moral conviction
- **Advocate Ranger:** Reconciling justice with wilderness pragmatism

## Species Considerations

- **Human Advocates:** Often driven by social reform and institutional justice
- **Dwarf Advocates:** Typically focused on tradition, honor, and ancestral codes
- **Elf Advocates:** Concerned with long-term moral consequences over centuries
- **Halfling Advocates:** Focused on community fairness and protection of common folk
