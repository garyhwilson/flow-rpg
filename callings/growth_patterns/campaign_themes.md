# Campaign Themes for Callings

## Introduction

Different Callings naturally resonate with different campaign themes, creating distinctive play experiences. This document explores how to design campaigns that highlight specific Callings and their motivations, providing GMs with a framework for creating compelling stories that engage players through their character's core drives.

## Thematic Campaigns by Calling

### Justice & Reformation (Advocate-Centered)
*"The world must be made right"*

#### Campaign Concepts
- **System Reformers:** Characters work to change corrupt institutions from within
- **Vigilante Justice:** When official channels fail, heroes take matters into their own hands
- **Truth Seekers:** Uncovering hidden corruption that threatens society
- **Moral Revolutionaries:** Challenging unjust traditions and practices

#### Key Elements
- **Central Conflicts:** Corruption vs. integrity, injustice vs. fairness
- **Recurring NPCs:** Corrupt officials, oppressed citizens, reformist allies
- **Settings:** Courts, bureaucracies, stratified societies, reform movements
- **Plot Structures:** Investigation → revelation → confrontation → reformation

#### Campaign Flow
Advocate-centered campaigns typically build Flow through exposing corruption and confronting wrongdoing. These campaigns excel at creating clear moral stakes that motivate decisive action.

#### Example Campaign
*"The Silver Scales"* - After discovering corruption in the capital city's justice system, the heroes work through layers of deception to expose a conspiracy reaching to the highest levels of government, ultimately reforming the system.

### Protection & Service (Guardian-Centered)
*"I exist to help others"*

#### Campaign Concepts
- **Community Defenders:** Protecting a settlement from escalating threats
- **Healing Mission:** Bringing aid to regions suffering from plague or disaster
- **Rescue Operations:** Saving those in peril across dangerous territories
- **Caretaker Heroes:** Building and maintaining havens of safety in harsh worlds

#### Key Elements
- **Central Conflicts:** Safety vs. danger, compassion vs. indifference
- **Recurring NPCs:** Vulnerable communities, those in need, dependent allies
- **Settings:** Threatened settlements, disaster zones, healing sanctuaries
- **Plot Structures:** Identify need → gather resources → overcome barriers → provide care

#### Campaign Flow
Guardian-centered campaigns generate Flow through successfully protecting others and responding to crises. These campaigns excel at creating emotional investment in NPCs and locations.

#### Example Campaign
*"Sanctuary"* - As a devastating plague spreads across the realm, the heroes establish and defend a network of healing sanctuaries while searching for a cure, balancing immediate protection with long-term solutions.

### Achievement & Recognition (Exemplar-Centered)
*"Success proves my worth"*

#### Campaign Concepts
- **Tournament Circuit:** Rising through competitive rankings toward championship
- **Legendary Quests:** Completing famous challenges to prove exceptional ability
- **Prestigious Organizations:** Earning membership in renowned guilds or orders
- **Legacy Builders:** Creating lasting achievements that will outlive the heroes

#### Key Elements
- **Central Conflicts:** Excellence vs. mediocrity, recognition vs. obscurity
- **Recurring NPCs:** Rivals, judges, potential mentors, adoring fans
- **Settings:** Arenas, exclusive organizations, historical sites of achievement
- **Plot Structures:** Challenge → preparation → test → recognition

#### Campaign Flow
Exemplar-centered campaigns build Flow through accomplishment and recognition. These campaigns excel at providing clear goals and measurable progress.

#### Example Campaign
*"The Immortal Circuit"* - The heroes enter a series of increasingly difficult magical tournaments, facing both honest competitors and corrupt influences, ultimately proving themselves among the greatest mages of the age.

### Identity & Discovery (Seeker-Centered)
*"I must find my true self"*

#### Campaign Concepts
- **Origin Mystery:** Uncovering forgotten pasts and hidden lineages
- **Transformation Journey:** Characters evolve through magical or spiritual changes
- **Authentic Creation:** Building something that truly expresses the inner self
- **Cultural Exploration:** Finding one's place between different worlds and traditions

#### Key Elements
- **Central Conflicts:** Authenticity vs. conformity, self-knowledge vs. denial
- **Recurring NPCs:** Those with answers about the past, mirrors of potential futures
- **Settings:** Places tied to origins, liminal spaces, transformative environments
- **Plot Structures:** Question → journey → revelation → integration

#### Campaign Flow
Seeker-centered campaigns generate Flow through authentic self-expression and personal discovery. These campaigns excel at character development and emotional depth.

#### Example Campaign
*"Echoes of the Forgotten"* - After discovering they share mysterious symbols on their skin, the heroes journey across realms to uncover their true origins, finding they are reincarnations of ancient guardians needed once again.

### Knowledge & Discovery (Scholar-Centered)
*"Knowledge reveals truth"*

#### Campaign Concepts
- **Ancient Mysteries:** Deciphering forgotten lore with modern implications
- **Arcane Research:** Developing new magical theories through experimentation
- **Expedition Scholars:** Documenting unexplored regions and phenomena
- **Knowledge Preservers:** Protecting crucial information from those who would destroy it

#### Key Elements
- **Central Conflicts:** Knowledge vs. ignorance, preservation vs. destruction
- **Recurring NPCs:** Rival researchers, knowledge keepers, dangerous innovators
- **Settings:** Libraries, laboratories, ancient ruins, unexplored territories
- **Plot Structures:** Question → research → expedition → discovery

#### Campaign Flow
Scholar-centered campaigns build Flow through research and discovery, with the unique ability to bank Flow between scenes. These campaigns excel at layered mysteries and intellectual challenges.

#### Example Campaign
*"The Forgotten Equations"* - Fragments of a world-changing magical formula are scattered in ancient ruins, and the heroes must piece them together while preventing misuse, ultimately deciding how this knowledge should be used.

### Security & Vigilance (Sentinel-Centered)
*"Loyalty and vigilance keep us safe"*

#### Campaign Concepts
- **Border Wardens:** Protecting realms from external threats
- **Conspiracy Hunters:** Uncovering hidden dangers within trusted institutions
- **Shield Bearers:** Defending important individuals or artifacts
- **Security Architects:** Building systems to protect against future dangers

#### Key Elements
- **Central Conflicts:** Safety vs. vulnerability, loyalty vs. betrayal
- **Recurring NPCs:** Potential threats, those who need protection, security allies
- **Settings:** Borders, defensive structures, infiltrated organizations
- **Plot Structures:** Warning signs → investigation → preventative action → security

#### Campaign Flow
Sentinel-centered campaigns generate Flow through vigilance and threat detection, with defensive bonuses. These campaigns excel at tension and proactive problem-solving.

#### Example Campaign
*"The Watching Wall"* - As guardians of an ancient barrier protecting the realm from otherworldly threats, the heroes must identify weakening points while uncovering traitors who seek to bring the wall down from within.

### Experience & Freedom (Enthusiast-Centered)
*"Life is an adventure to experience"*

#### Campaign Concepts
- **Grand Tour:** Experiencing the wonders of diverse cultures and locations
- **Freedom Fighters:** Liberating people from oppressive restrictions
- **Joy Bringers:** Restoring happiness to places overcome by gloom
- **Possibility Creators:** Opening new options where none existed before

#### Key Elements
- **Central Conflicts:** Freedom vs. constraint, joy vs. suffering
- **Recurring NPCs:** Fellow experience-seekers, those trapped by circumstance
- **Settings:** Diverse and sensory-rich environments, repressive regimes
- **Plot Structures:** Discover opportunity → overcome limitation → create possibility → celebrate

#### Campaign Flow
Enthusiast-centered campaigns generate Flow through new experiences and opportunity creation, with unique Flow-sharing abilities. These campaigns excel at variety and positive emotion.

#### Example Campaign
*"The Unwritten Roads"* - The heroes discover magical pathways to forgotten wonders, freeing communities from isolation while protecting these experiences from those who would commercialize and control them.

### Strength & Protection (Champion-Centered)
*"Strength protects what matters"*

#### Campaign Concepts
- **Bulwark Heroes:** Standing between civilization and overwhelming threats
- **Power Seekers:** Gaining strength to protect what matters most
- **Liberation Forces:** Using power to free the oppressed
- **Worthy Leaders:** Proving the right to lead through strength of character

#### Key Elements
- **Central Conflicts:** Strength vs. weakness, protection vs. vulnerability
- **Recurring NPCs:** Those in need of protection, power-abusers, worthy rivals
- **Settings:** Frontlines, threatened regions, arenas of power
- **Plot Structures:** Identify threat → gather strength → decisive confrontation → establish safety

#### Campaign Flow
Champion-centered campaigns build Flow through protective action in aggressive stance. These campaigns excel at climactic confrontations and decisive action.

#### Example Campaign
*"The Iron Covenant"* - When ancient titans begin awakening to reclaim the world, the heroes must master powerful artifacts to become champions capable of protecting civilization from these overwhelming forces.

### Harmony & Connection (Mediator-Centered)
*"Harmony brings peace to all"*

#### Campaign Concepts
- **Peace Forgers:** Creating accords between hostile factions
- **Balance Keepers:** Maintaining harmony between opposing forces
- **Cultural Bridges:** Helping different groups understand each other
- **Conflict Healers:** Addressing the root causes of recurring strife

#### Key Elements
- **Central Conflicts:** Unity vs. division, understanding vs. prejudice
- **Recurring NPCs:** Representatives of different factions, conflict victims
- **Settings:** Borderlands, diplomatic venues, divided communities
- **Plot Structures:** Understand conflict → build connections → facilitate dialogue → create harmony

#### Campaign Flow
Mediator-centered campaigns generate Flow through successful conflict resolution. These campaigns excel at complex social dynamics and nuanced solutions.

#### Example Campaign
*"The Concordant Realms"* - After centuries of war between elemental domains threatens to destroy reality itself, the heroes must understand each realm's perspective and forge a new balance that respects all elements.

## Mixed-Calling Campaign Designs

Most campaigns will feature characters with different Callings. These structures accommodate diverse motivations:

### Balanced Approach
- **Design Philosophy:** Include elements appealing to each Calling present
- **Structure:** Rotating focus on different Calling motivations
- **Flow Dynamics:** Multiple ways to generate Flow in most scenarios
- **Character Development:** Each Calling has opportunities to shine

#### Example Implementation
If your party includes an Advocate, Guardian, and Scholar, design quests that involve:
- Uncovering corruption (Advocate)
- Protecting vulnerable people (Guardian)
- Researching crucial information (Scholar)

### Thematic Focus with Multiple Entry Points
- **Design Philosophy:** Central theme with different aspects relevant to each Calling
- **Structure:** Shared mission with diverse approaches
- **Flow Dynamics:** Complementary Flow generation
- **Character Development:** Different perspectives on same issues

#### Example Implementation
A campaign about a magical plague could engage:
- Exemplar: Proving themselves by finding a cure
- Guardian: Protecting and healing the afflicted
- Sentinel: Preventing the plague from spreading
- Scholar: Researching the plague's origins and mechanisms

### Internal Tension Design
- **Design Philosophy:** Deliberately incorporate conflicts between Calling motivations
- **Structure:** Scenarios requiring Calling compromises
- **Flow Dynamics:** Tension between different Flow generation methods
- **Character Development:** Growth through challenging core drives

#### Example Implementation
A mission to infiltrate a corrupt but charitable noble house creates tension for:
- Advocate: Exposing corruption vs. maintaining charitable works
- Mediator: Finding harmony vs. confronting wrongdoing
- Seeker: Authentic action vs. necessary deception
- Champion: Protecting innocents vs. confronting authority

## Campaign Elements by Calling

### Objectives That Resonate
Different Callings are motivated by different types of goals:

- **Advocate:** Expose corruption, establish justice, reform broken systems
- **Guardian:** Protect the vulnerable, heal the wounded, preserve communities
- **Exemplar:** Achieve recognition, master skills, create lasting works
- **Seeker:** Discover identity, express authenticity, find belonging
- **Scholar:** Uncover knowledge, solve mysteries, preserve information
- **Sentinel:** Ensure security, maintain vigilance, honor loyalties
- **Enthusiast:** Experience wonders, spread joy, create possibilities
- **Champion:** Demonstrate strength, overcome challenges, establish order
- **Mediator:** Create harmony, resolve conflicts, build understanding

### Rewards That Matter
Beyond material treasure, different Callings value different rewards:

- **Advocate:** Recognition of moral rightness, systemic changes, justice served
- **Guardian:** Thriving communities, gratitude, relationships
- **Exemplar:** Titles, commendations, measureable achievements
- **Seeker:** Self-knowledge, unique experiences, authenticity acknowledgment
- **Scholar:** Rare information, research opportunities, expanded understanding
- **Sentinel:** Security improvements, loyalty recognition, threat elimination
- **Enthusiast:** New possibilities, freedom enhancements, joy creation
- **Champion:** Respect for strength, leadership opportunities, protected domains
- **Mediator:** Peaceful resolutions, new connections, balanced solutions

### Villains That Challenge
The most compelling antagonists challenge a Calling's core values:

- **Advocate Villains:** Corrupt officials who pervert justice, moral relativists
- **Guardian Villains:** Those who exploit the vulnerable, destroyers of community
- **Exemplar Villains:** Achievement through any means, fame-stealers
- **Seeker Villains:** Identity suppressors, conformity enforcers, manipulators
- **Scholar Villains:** Knowledge hoarders, book burners, deliberate obscurantists
- **Sentinel Villains:** Betrayers, security underminders, loyalty testers
- **Enthusiast Villains:** Joy destroyers, freedom limiters, pessimism spreaders
- **Champion Villains:** Strength abusers, oppressive tyrants, control addicts
- **Mediator Villains:** Conflict instigators, divisive influences, extremists

## Practical Campaign Design

### Session Planning for Callings
Structure sessions to engage different Callings:

- **Opening Hook:** Appeal to primary Calling motivations
- **Investigation/Exploration:** Include multiple paths aligned with different Callings
- **Complication:** Challenge Calling assumptions
- **Climax:** Allow complementary Callings to shine together
- **Resolution:** Rewards that matter to each Calling

### Campaign Arcs by Calling

#### Short Campaigns (3-5 sessions)
- **Advocate:** Expose and address a specific injustice
- **Guardian:** Protect a community from an immediate threat
- **Exemplar:** Complete a specific prestigious achievement
- **Seeker:** Discover one important aspect of identity
- **Scholar:** Solve a focused mystery with clear parameters
- **Sentinel:** Identify and neutralize a specific threat
- **Enthusiast:** Experience a particular wonder or adventure
- **Champion:** Overcome a direct challenger or threat
- **Mediator:** Resolve a specific conflict between two parties

#### Medium Campaigns (10-20 sessions)
- **Advocate:** Reform a corrupt institution
- **Guardian:** Build sustainable protection for a region
- **Exemplar:** Rise through ranks of competitive hierarchy
- **Seeker:** Undergo significant personal transformation
- **Scholar:** Uncover multi-layered knowledge with applications
- **Sentinel:** Address complex security challenges
- **Enthusiast:** Create new possibilities in restricted environments
- **Champion:** Develop strength to address growing threats
- **Mediator:** Create understanding between multiple factions

#### Long Campaigns (20+ sessions)
- **Advocate:** Transform societal values and systems
- **Guardian:** Create lasting sanctuary amid large-scale threats
- **Exemplar:** Achieve legendary status with permanent impact
- **Seeker:** Complete fundamental journey of self-discovery
- **Scholar:** Revolutionize understanding of crucial knowledge
- **Sentinel:** Establish new security paradigms
- **Enthusiast:** Liberate entire regions from limitation
- **Champion:** Become the definitive protection against ultimate threats
- **Mediator:** Forge harmony between fundamentally opposed forces

### Flow Economy by Campaign Type
Different campaign themes create distinctive Flow patterns:

- **Combat-Heavy Campaigns:** Favor Champion, Sentinel, Guardian
- **Social Intrigue Campaigns:** Favor Mediator, Advocate, Exemplar
- **Exploration Campaigns:** Favor Enthusiast, Seeker, Scholar
- **Mystery Campaigns:** Favor Scholar, Sentinel, Advocate
- **Diplomatic Campaigns:** Favor Mediator, Guardian, Exemplar

Ensure campaigns include multiple Flow generation opportunities to engage all Callings present in your party.

## Conclusion

By designing campaigns that resonate with character Callings, GMs create more engaging experiences where player motivation aligns naturally with story progression. Whether focusing on a specific Calling theme or integrating multiple motivations, understanding these core drives provides a framework for meaningful adventures that feel personally relevant to each character's journey.
