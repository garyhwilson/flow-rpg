# Callings System Integration

## Milestones and Callings

The milestone advancement system naturally pairs with the Calling system, as character growth often aligns with Calling development.

### Earning Milestones Through Your Calling

Milestones emerge naturally from pursuing your Calling. Small moments of character expression earn minor rewards. Significant challenges that test your convictions warrant moderate advancement. Transformative events that fundamentally change who you are - or prove who you've always been - represent major milestones.

Here's how this plays out for different Callings:

**Advocate:**

- **Minor**: Successfully exposing a minor injustice
- **Moderate**: Reforming a corrupt system
- **Major**: Bringing a major villain to justice at great personal cost

**Guardian:**

- **Minor**: Protecting someone from immediate harm
- **Moderate**: Making a significant sacrifice to help others
- **Major**: Establishing a lasting protection for a community

**Exemplar:**

- **Minor**: Achieving recognition for exceptional skill
- **Moderate**: Winning a significant competition or challenge
- **Major**: Reaching the pinnacle of achievement in their field

**Seeker:**

- **Minor**: Discovering a new aspect of their identity
- **Moderate**: Taking a stand based on personal truth despite opposition
- **Major**: Fundamental transformation of self-understanding

**Scholar:**

- **Minor**: Uncovering valuable knowledge
- **Moderate**: Applying knowledge to solve a complex problem
- **Major**: Making a groundbreaking discovery

**Sentinel:**

- **Minor**: Successfully preventing a threat through preparation
- **Moderate**: Proving loyalty despite personal cost
- **Major**: Establishing lasting security for something they protect

**Enthusiast:**

- **Minor**: Finding joy in a difficult situation
- **Moderate**: Helping others embrace freedom and possibility
- **Major**: Overcoming a profound limitation to experience life fully

**Champion:**

- **Minor**: Successfully defeating a worthy opponent
- **Moderate**: Protecting others through strength and leadership
- **Major**: Overcoming a fundamental weakness to become stronger

**Mediator:**

- **Minor**: Successfully resolving a conflict
- **Moderate**: Bringing hostile groups to peaceful agreement
- **Major**: Creating lasting harmony between long-standing enemies

## Momentum and Callings

The Momentum system is deeply integrated with Callings, creating a resource economy that reinforces character motivation.

### Momentum Generation through Callings

Each Calling generates Momentum through actions aligned with their core drive:

**Advocate:** Exposing corruption or injustice (+1 Momentum)
**Guardian:** When allies become seriously injured (+1 Momentum)
**Exemplar:** Critical success toward declared goals (+2 Momentum)
**Seeker:** Authentic unconventional actions (+1 Momentum)
**Scholar:** Knowledge-based actions (can bank up to 2 Momentum)
**Sentinel:** Any Momentum gain also adds +1 Guard
**Enthusiast:** Can transfer gained Momentum to allies
**Champion:** Extra Momentum in Aggressive stance when protecting (+1 Momentum)
**Mediator:** Resolving conflicts peacefully (+1 Momentum)

### How Each Calling Spends Momentum

Your Calling shapes not just when you gain Momentum, but how you instinctively want to spend it.

**Advocates** save their Momentum for moments of revelation. When Marcus the Advocate finally confronts the corrupt magistrate, he pours 3 Momentum into _Righteous Revelation_ - the truth spills out whether the magistrate wants it to or not. Every coin skimmed, every bribe taken, laid bare.

**Guardians** spend Momentum protecting others. Sera sees the arrow flying toward the young prince and burns 2 Momentum on _Guardian's Intercession_, throwing herself into its path. The wound hurts, but the prince lives.

**Exemplars** invest Momentum in moments that will be remembered. Kael the duelist saves his Momentum for the climactic exchange, then spends 3 Momentum on _Perfect Performance_ - his bladework becomes poetry, witnessed by the assembled nobles.

**Seekers** spend Momentum to be truly themselves. When the priest demands Lyra conform to tradition, she burns 2 Momentum on _True Self_, speaking her mind despite the social pressure that would normally silence her.

**Scholars** hoard Momentum for critical insights. Brother Aldric has been saving Momentum all session. When the party needs to decipher the ancient text, he spends 2 Momentum on _Perfect Recall_, remembering a footnote from a book he read twenty years ago.

**Sentinels** spend Momentum on vigilance. Captain Thorne burns 3 Momentum on _Perfect Vigilance_ before entering the warehouse - every trap, every hidden enemy, every escape route revealed to his keen awareness.

**Enthusiasts** share their Momentum freely. Pip spends 2 Momentum on _Boundless Optimism_ to lift the despair from a wounded companion - their pain doesn't disappear, but suddenly it feels bearable.

**Champions** spend Momentum on decisive action. When the enemy champion stands between her and victory, Valeria pours 4 Momentum into _Overwhelming Force_ - one strike that ends the duel.

**Mediators** spend Momentum on understanding. Ambassador Kai burns 3 Momentum on _Perfect Understanding_ - suddenly the trade negotiation makes sense, the hidden agendas clear, the path to agreement obvious.

## Combat Stances and Callings

The stance-based combat system interacts uniquely with each Calling, creating distinctive tactical options.

### Stance Preferences

Different Callings naturally gravitate toward certain combat stances:

**Advocate:**

- **Preferred Stance:** Balanced (flexibility to respond to injustice)
- **Secondary Stance:** Aggressive (when confronting corruption)
- **Momentum Tactics:** Generates Momentum in any stance when exposing wrongdoing

**Guardian:**

- **Preferred Stance:** Defensive (protection oriented)
- **Secondary Stance:** Balanced (flexibility to react to threats)
- **Momentum Tactics:** Protective Surge triggers regardless of stance

**Exemplar:**

- **Preferred Stance:** Aggressive (demonstrating prowess)
- **Secondary Stance:** Balanced (adaptability for success)
- **Momentum Tactics:** Excellence Cascade works in any stance

**Seeker:**

- **Preferred Stance:** Varies (changes with self-discovery)
- **Secondary Stance:** Balanced (freedom to express)
- **Momentum Tactics:** Authentic Action rewards unconventional stance choices

**Scholar:**

- **Preferred Stance:** Balanced (observation and analysis)
- **Secondary Stance:** Defensive (safety to study)
- **Momentum Tactics:** Banks Momentum generated in any stance for later use

**Sentinel:**

- **Preferred Stance:** Defensive (vigilant protection)
- **Secondary Stance:** Balanced (readiness to respond)
- **Momentum Tactics:** Defensive Vigilance adds Guard when gaining Momentum

**Enthusiast:**

- **Preferred Stance:** Aggressive (experiencing excitement)
- **Secondary Stance:** Balanced (freedom to adapt)
- **Momentum Tactics:** Joyful Momentum works regardless of stance

**Champion:**

- **Preferred Stance:** Aggressive (demonstrating strength)
- **Secondary Stance:** Defensive (when protecting others)
- **Momentum Tactics:** Protective Dominance specifically works in Aggressive stance

**Mediator:**

- **Preferred Stance:** Defensive (avoiding unnecessary conflict)
- **Secondary Stance:** Balanced (adapting to situation)
- **Momentum Tactics:** Often seeks non-violent resolutions that generate Momentum

### Stance Switching Strategies

Calling influences when and why characters switch stances:

**Advocate:** Switches to Aggressive when confronting corruption, Defensive when protecting victims
**Guardian:** Stays Defensive to protect others, switches to Balanced when allies are safe
**Exemplar:** Switches to whatever stance showcases their abilities best for the situation
**Seeker:** Switches stances experimentally to discover effective approaches
**Scholar:** Stays Balanced to observe, switches based on analytical conclusions
**Sentinel:** Starts Defensive, switches to Balanced once threats are assessed
**Enthusiast:** Frequently switches stances to experience different approaches
**Champion:** Stays Aggressive when protecting, switches to Balanced for tactical advantage
**Mediator:** Stays Defensive or Balanced, rarely uses Aggressive except as last resort

## Zone Movement and Positioning

Callings influence how characters position themselves in combat zones:

**Advocate:** Positions to confront leaders and sources of corruption
**Guardian:** Stays Close to vulnerable allies, between them and threats
**Exemplar:** Positions for maximum visibility and impact
**Seeker:** Positions unpredictably, often in unique tactical locations
**Scholar:** Maintains distance to observe overall patterns
**Sentinel:** Controls access points and approaches
**Enthusiast:** Moves frequently, experiencing different tactical positions
**Champion:** Positions at Engaged range with primary threats
**Mediator:** Positions to facilitate communication between combatants

## Magic and Callings

### Magical Style by Calling

**Advocate:**

- Favors Mind casting for precise justice
- Spells that reveal truth and expose corruption
- Magic that binds the guilty and protects the innocent

**Guardian:**

- Favors Will casting for reliable protection
- Healing and defensive magic
- Spells that shield others from harm

**Exemplar:**

- Favors Presence casting for impressive displays
- Magic that demonstrates skill and capability
- Spells with visible, recognition-worthy effects

**Seeker:**

- Varies casting attribute based on personal journey
- Unique, personalized magical effects
- Magic that reveals hidden aspects of self and others

**Scholar:**

- Favors Mind casting for precise application
- Analytical, carefully controlled magic
- Spells that gather or preserve knowledge

**Sentinel:**

- Favors Awareness casting for threat detection
- Warning and protective magic
- Spells that secure areas and prevent intrusion

**Enthusiast:**

- Favors Presence casting for joyful expression
- Vibrant, experiential magic
- Spells that create opportunities and possibilities

**Champion:**

- Favors Will casting for forceful application
- Powerful, dominating magic
- Spells that enhance strength and overcome obstacles

**Mediator:**

- Favors Awareness casting for understanding
- Harmonizing, connecting magic
- Spells that facilitate communication and peace

## Equipment Preferences

Callings influence equipment selection and usage:

**Advocate:** Justice symbols, recording equipment, evidence kits
**Guardian:** Medical supplies, shields, defensive gear
**Exemplar:** High-quality, distinctive equipment that showcases skill
**Seeker:** Unique, personally meaningful items, often modified conventional gear
**Scholar:** Books, writing materials, specialized research tools
**Sentinel:** Alarm systems, traps, surveillance equipment
**Enthusiast:** Versatile, multi-purpose equipment for diverse experiences
**Champion:** Impressive weapons, armor with presence, symbols of authority
**Mediator:** Communication aids, cultural symbols, diplomatic credentials

## Character Sheet Integration

On character sheets, Callings should be prominently featured:

1. **Calling Section:** Dedicated area showing Calling name, Momentum Benefit, and Narrative Permission
2. **Momentum Tracking:** Space to note Calling-specific Momentum generation
3. **Narrative Permission Tracker:** Checkboxes for once-per-scene permissions
4. **Milestone Connection:** Space to record how milestone advancement relates to Calling development

_Always remember that while Callings have mechanical effects, their primary purpose is to guide roleplaying and character development. The mechanical benefits should reinforce the narrative aspects rather than overshadow them._
