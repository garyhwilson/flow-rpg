## Milestones and Callings

The milestone advancement system naturally pairs with the Calling system, as character growth often aligns with Calling development.

### Earning Milestones Through Your Calling

Milestones emerge naturally from pursuing your Calling. Small moments of character expression earn minor rewards. Significant challenges that test your convictions warrant moderate advancement. Transformative events that fundamentally change who you are - or prove who you've always been - represent major milestones.

Here's how this plays out for different Callings:

**Advocate:**
- **Minor**: Successfully exposing a minor injustice
- **Moderate**: Reforming a corrupt system
- **Major**: Bringing a major villain to justice at great personal cost

**Guardian:**
- **Minor**: Protecting someone from immediate harm
- **Moderate**: Making a significant sacrifice to help others
- **Major**: Establishing a lasting protection for a community

**Exemplar:**
- **Minor**: Achieving recognition for exceptional skill
- **Moderate**: Winning a significant competition or challenge
- **Major**: Reaching the pinnacle of achievement in their field

**Seeker:**
- **Minor**: Discovering a new aspect of their identity
- **Moderate**: Taking a stand based on personal truth despite opposition
- **Major**: Fundamental transformation of self-understanding

**Scholar:**
- **Minor**: Uncovering valuable knowledge
- **Moderate**: Applying knowledge to solve a complex problem
- **Major**: Making a groundbreaking discovery

**Sentinel:**
- **Minor**: Successfully preventing a threat through preparation
- **Moderate**: Proving loyalty despite personal cost
- **Major**: Establishing lasting security for something they protect

**Enthusiast:**
- **Minor**: Finding joy in a difficult situation
- **Moderate**: Helping others embrace freedom and possibility
- **Major**: Overcoming a profound limitation to experience life fully

**Champion:**
- **Minor**: Successfully defeating a worthy opponent
- **Moderate**: Protecting others through strength and leadership
- **Major**: Overcoming a fundamental weakness to become stronger

**Mediator:**
- **Minor**: Successfully resolving a conflict
- **Moderate**: Bringing hostile groups to peaceful agreement
- **Major**: Creating lasting harmony between long-standing enemies

## Flow and Callings

The Flow system is deeply integrated with Callings, creating a resource economy that reinforces character motivation.

### Flow Generation through Callings

Each Calling generates Flow through actions aligned with their core drive:

**Advocate:** Exposing corruption or injustice (+1 Flow)
**Guardian:** When allies become seriously injured (+1 Flow)
**Exemplar:** Critical success toward declared goals (+2 Flow)
**Seeker:** Authentic unconventional actions (+1 Flow)
**Scholar:** Knowledge-based actions (can bank up to 2 Flow)
**Sentinel:** Any Flow gain also adds +1 Guard
**Enthusiast:** Can transfer gained Flow to allies
**Champion:** Extra Flow in Aggressive stance when protecting (+1 Flow)
**Mediator:** Resolving conflicts peacefully (+1 Flow)

### How Each Calling Spends Flow

Your Calling shapes not just when you gain Flow, but how you instinctively want to spend it.

**Advocates** save their Flow for moments of revelation. When Marcus the Advocate finally confronts the corrupt magistrate, he pours 3 Flow into *Righteous Revelation* - the truth spills out whether the magistrate wants it to or not. Every coin skimmed, every bribe taken, laid bare.

**Guardians** spend Flow protecting others. Sera sees the arrow flying toward the young prince and burns 2 Flow on *Guardian's Intercession*, throwing herself into its path. The wound hurts, but the prince lives.

**Exemplars** invest Flow in moments that will be remembered. Kael the duelist saves his Flow for the climactic exchange, then spends 3 Flow on *Perfect Performance* - his bladework becomes poetry, witnessed by the assembled nobles.

**Seekers** spend Flow to be truly themselves. When the priest demands Lyra conform to tradition, she burns 2 Flow on *True Self*, speaking her mind despite the social pressure that would normally silence her.

**Scholars** hoard Flow for critical insights. Brother Aldric has been saving Flow all session. When the party needs to decipher the ancient text, he spends 2 Flow on *Perfect Recall*, remembering a footnote from a book he read twenty years ago.

**Sentinels** spend Flow on vigilance. Captain Thorne burns 3 Flow on *Perfect Vigilance* before entering the warehouse - every trap, every hidden enemy, every escape route revealed to his keen awareness.

**Enthusiasts** share their Flow freely. Pip spends 2 Flow on *Boundless Optimism* to lift the despair from a wounded companion - their pain doesn't disappear, but suddenly it feels bearable.

**Champions** spend Flow on decisive action. When the enemy champion stands between her and victory, Valeria pours 4 Flow into *Overwhelming Force* - one strike that ends the duel.

**Mediators** spend Flow on understanding. Ambassador Kai burns 3 Flow on *Perfect Understanding* - suddenly the trade negotiation makes sense, the hidden agendas clear, the path to agreement obvious.

## Combat Stances and Callings

The stance-based combat system interacts uniquely with each Calling, creating distinctive tactical options.

### Stance Preferences

Different Callings naturally gravitate toward certain combat stances:

**Advocate:**
- **Preferred Stance:** Balanced (flexibility to respond to injustice)
- **Secondary Stance:** Aggressive (when confronting corruption)
- **Flow Tactics:** Generates Flow in any stance when exposing wrongdoing

**Guardian:**
- **Preferred Stance:** Defensive (protection oriented)
- **Secondary Stance:** Balanced (flexibility to react to threats)
- **Flow Tactics:** Protective Surge triggers regardless of stance

**Exemplar:**
- **Preferred Stance:** Aggressive (demonstrating prowess)
- **Secondary Stance:** Balanced (adaptability for success)
- **Flow Tactics:** Excellence Cascade works in any stance

**Seeker:**
- **Preferred Stance:** Varies (changes with self-discovery)
- **Secondary Stance:** Balanced (freedom to express)
- **Flow Tactics:** Authentic Action rewards unconventional stance choices

**Scholar:**
- **Preferred Stance:** Balanced (observation and analysis)
- **Secondary Stance:** Defensive (safety to study)
- **Flow Tactics:** Banks Flow generated in any stance for later use

**Sentinel:**
- **Preferred Stance:** Defensive (vigilant protection)
- **Secondary Stance:** Balanced (readiness to respond)
- **Flow Tactics:** Defensive Vigilance adds Guard when gaining Flow

**Enthusiast:**
- **Preferred Stance:** Aggressive (experiencing excitement)
- **Secondary Stance:** Balanced (freedom to adapt)
- **Flow Tactics:** Joyful Flow works regardless of stance

**Champion:**
- **Preferred Stance:** Aggressive (demonstrating strength)
- **Secondary Stance:** Defensive (when protecting others)
- **Flow Tactics:** Protective Dominance specifically works in Aggressive stance

**Mediator:**
- **Preferred Stance:** Defensive (avoiding unnecessary conflict)
- **Secondary Stance:** Balanced (adapting to situation)
- **Flow Tactics:** Often seeks non-violent resolutions that generate Flow

### Stance Switching Strategies

Calling influences when and why characters switch stances:

**Advocate:** Switches to Aggressive when confronting corruption, Defensive when protecting victims
**Guardian:** Stays Defensive to protect others, switches to Balanced when allies are safe
**Exemplar:** Switches to whatever stance showcases their abilities best for the situation
**Seeker:** Switches stances experimentally to discover effective approaches
**Scholar:** Stays Balanced to observe, switches based on analytical conclusions
**Sentinel:** Starts Defensive, switches to Balanced once threats are assessed
**Enthusiast:** Frequently switches stances to experience different approaches
**Champion:** Stays Aggressive when protecting, switches to Balanced for tactical advantage
**Mediator:** Stays Defensive or Balanced, rarely uses Aggressive except as last resort

## Zone Movement and Positioning

Callings influence how characters position themselves in combat zones:

**Advocate:** Positions to confront leaders and sources of corruption
**Guardian:** Stays Close to vulnerable allies, between them and threats
**Exemplar:** Positions for maximum visibility and impact
**Seeker:** Positions unpredictably, often in unique tactical locations
**Scholar:** Maintains distance to observe overall patterns
**Sentinel:** Controls access points and approaches
**Enthusiast:** Moves frequently, experiencing different tactical positions
**Champion:** Positions at Engaged range with primary threats
**Mediator:** Positions to facilitate communication between combatants

**Enthusiast:**
- Favors Presence casting for joyful expression

**Champion:**

Callings influence equipment selection and usage:

**Guardian:** Medical supplies, shields, defensive gear
**Exemplar:** High-quality, distinctive equipment that showcases skill
**Seeker:** Unique, personally meaningful items, often modified conventional gear
**Scholar:** Books, writing materials, specialized research tools

**Champion:** Impressive weapons, armor with presence, symbols of authority

## Character Sheet Integration

On character sheets, Callings should be prominently featured:

1. **Calling Section:** Dedicated area showing Calling name, Flow Benefit, and Narrative Permission
2. **Flow Tracking:** Space to note Calling-specific Flow generation
3. **Narrative Permission Tracker:** Checkboxes for once-per-scene permissions
4. **Milestone Connection:** Space to record how milestone advancement relates to Calling development

*Always remember that while Callings have mechanical effects, their primary purpose is to guide roleplaying and character development. The mechanical benefits should reinforce the narrative aspects rather than overshadow them.*
