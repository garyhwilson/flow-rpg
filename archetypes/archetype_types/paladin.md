# Paladin
*Channeling Conviction into Protective Capabilities, Standing as Bulwarks Against Threats*

## Archetype Essence

Paladins represent the embodiment of sacred principles through martial discipline, individuals who have transformed personal conviction into physical power through unwavering dedication. Unlike those who fight merely for pay, glory, or necessity, true Paladins approach combat as sacred duty—a physical manifestation of their commitment to principles transcending individual concerns.

At their core, Paladins are living vessels who translate abstract moral codes into tangible protective action. When faced with adversity, they rely on unwavering conviction, physical capability, and the manifestation of their oath-bound power rather than expedient compromise or pragmatic adjustment. This approach stems from their foundational belief that some principles must be defended regardless of cost, and that the physical protection of the vulnerable represents spiritual duty as much as practical necessity.

The Paladin tradition evolved from early oath-sworn champions and sacred defenders into formalized orders incorporating diverse ethical codes across cultures. While specific principles and manifestations vary dramatically, all Paladins share a fundamental commitment to embodying their chosen values through direct action and personal sacrifice. This profession attracts individuals with profound moral clarity, physical capability, and the courage to stand as living examples of their beliefs even when facing overwhelming opposition.

### Historical Development

The Paladin archetype emerged independently across cultures as societies recognized that certain virtues required more than mere philosophical articulation—they needed physical defenders willing to risk everything for their preservation. Early practitioners developed oath-based combat systems, pioneering training methodologies that could transform moral conviction into tangible power through disciplined integration of spiritual principle and martial technique.

These traditions evolved from simple warrior codes into sophisticated systems with theological foundations, martial methodologies, and institutional structures. By codifying effective approaches to oath-bound defense, Paladin traditions enabled knowledge transmission across generations, creating cumulative refinement of both ethical understanding and practical application.

Today's Paladins inherit traditions representing centuries of development, with approaches tested and validated across countless conflicts. While some traditionalists maintain historical codes unchanged, many contemporary practitioners adapt ancient principles to address modern challenges, creating innovative interpretations that maintain core values while responding to evolving threats.

## Mechanical Foundations

### Key Attributes

Paladins typically emphasize spiritual fortitude and physical capability, though specific attribute priorities vary based on oath focus and cultural tradition:

**Will:** Primary attribute for many Paladins, supporting unwavering conviction, oath maintenance, and resistance to corrupting influences.

**Might:** Emphasized by Paladins focused on direct protection, physical interposition, and martial capability.

**Presence:** Crucial for embodying principles through personal example, inspiring allies, and manifesting oath authority.

**Awareness:** Valued for perceiving threats to protected values, identifying moral challenges, and maintaining spiritual vigilance.

**Mind:** Supports ethical understanding, tactical application of limited resources, and comprehension of complex moral situations.

**Grace:** Typically receives less emphasis, though some traditions incorporate ceremonial movement or precise martial techniques requiring physical coordination.

Most Paladins develop at least moderate scores in Will and either Might or Presence, with secondary emphasis reflecting specific oath focus or cultural tradition.

### Starting Skills

Paladins begin with the following skill proficiencies, reflecting their specialized training in principle-guided combat and protective service:

**Professional (+1):**
- Command (inspirational leadership, authoritative presence, and decisive direction)

**Competent (+0):**
- Combat (martial techniques, defensive positioning, and protective tactics)

**Novice (-1):**
- Medicine (treatment of battle injuries, particularly for protected individuals)
- Sorcery (divine magic channeling for protective and smiting effects)

**Special Ability:**
- **Divine Spellcasting:** Can cast divine spells regardless of attributes (archetype training overrides normal spellcasting requirements)

**Additional Skills:**
- Choose three additional skills at Basic/Untrained level reflecting background and cultural training

Paladins are typically Untrained in deceptive practices, self-serving activities, or any skills contradicting their sworn principles unless their background specifically includes exposure to these areas before taking their oaths.

### Flow Mechanics

Paladins interact with the Flow resource through principled action and oath fulfillment:

**Flow Generation:**
- Acting in accordance with sworn principles despite personal cost (+1 Flow)
- Successfully protecting vulnerable individuals or values (+1 Flow)
- Confronting significant threats to sacred principles (+1 Flow)
- Inspiring others through principled example (+1 Flow)
- Overcoming temptations to compromise core values (+2 Flow)

**Flow Expenditure:**
- Manifesting oath-bound power (costs vary by manifestation type)
- Enhancing protective capabilities (+1 Flow for additional effect)
- Establishing sanctified spaces (+1 Flow for area protection)
- Detecting threats to protected principles (+1 Flow for enhanced perception)
- Manifesting extraordinary oath fulfillment (+2 Flow for significant effects)

Paladins typically maintain stable Flow reserves through consistent adherence to principles, then spend it on manifestations of their oath-bound power during moments of critical need. This pattern reflects their approach of steadfast preparation followed by decisive action when principles face genuine threat.

### Signature Techniques

Paladins develop specialized techniques reflecting their oath-bound approach to protection:

**Protective Interposition (Flow +1):** Place yourself between danger and vulnerable individuals, channeling oath power into enhanced defensive capabilities. This technique exemplifies the Paladin's core role as physical manifestation of protective principles.

**Smite (Flow +2):** Channel conviction directly into overwhelming attack against threats to sworn principles, manifesting righteous judgment through focused combat application. This technique demonstrates the Paladin's capability to serve as both judge and executioner when principles demand direct opposition.

**Oath Presence (Flow +1):** Manifest the spiritual weight of sworn principles, enhancing social authority through genuine conviction. This technique reflects the Paladin's status as living embodiment of abstract values made tangible through personal commitment.

**Sanctifying Aura (Flow +2):** Extend protective influence beyond personal boundaries, creating consecrated space that strengthens allies while hindering malevolent forces. This technique illustrates the Paladin's ability to transform surrounding environment through projected conviction.

**Unwavering Resolve (Flow +1):** Maintain effectiveness despite overwhelming adversity through perfect alignment with sworn principles. This technique showcases the Paladin's defining characteristic—their capacity to continue functioning through challenges that would break individuals lacking their moral clarity.

## Training & Mentorship

Paladin training follows distinctive patterns reflecting the integration of martial discipline with spiritual development:

### Identification of Potential

Most Paladin traditions recognize that effective oath-bearers require innate qualities beyond what training alone can develop. Identification methods include:

**Virtue Testing:** Many traditions subject candidates to graduated moral challenges revealing character quality under pressure, including temptation resistance, courage demonstration, and integrity verification under adverse conditions.

**Combat Aptitude:** Physical capability assessment determining potential effectiveness as principle defender, including strength evaluation, endurance testing, and tactical decision-making under simulated combat conditions.

**Spiritual Resonance:** Evaluation of candidate connection with tradition's foundational principles through ritual exposure, meditation assessment, or guided contemplation revealing genuine affinity beyond intellectual agreement.

**Crisis Performance:** Observation during unexpected challenges revealing natural inclinations toward protective service, leadership assumption, or principled decision-making without preparation opportunity.

**Lineage Consideration:** Some traditions, particularly those emphasizing hereditary responsibility, examine family history for indicators of suitable temperament or established service patterns suggesting inherent compatibility.

### Training Methodologies

Once identified, potential Paladins undergo specialized training developing both physical capability and spiritual alignment:

**Integrated Development:** Unlike traditions separating martial and spiritual training, effective Paladin methodologies interweave physical discipline with principle internalization, demonstrating inseparable connection between combat technique and ethical foundation.

**Progressive Oaths:** Many traditions implement graduated commitment system beginning with limited obligations and advancing toward comprehensive life dedication, allowing progressive testing before ultimate commitment.

**Physical Consecration:** Training regimens incorporating ritualized elements transforming ordinary exercise into sacred discipline, including blessing ceremonies, meditative components, or symbolic representations within training equipment.

**Simulated Ethical Challenges:** Constructed scenarios testing principle application within ambiguous situations, developing discernment capabilities distinguishing genuine virtue expression from mere rule following.

**Community Service:** Practical application of protective principles through actual community assistance, demonstrating tangible benefits of abstract values while developing appropriate humility through direct contact with protected populations.

### Mentor-Aspirant Relationships

The relationship between established Paladins and their trainees forms crucial component of effective development:

**Living Example:** Beyond mere instruction, effective mentors embody oath principles through personal conduct, demonstrating abstract concepts through observable behavior reinforcing verbal teaching.

**Guided Reflection:** Experienced Paladins help aspirants process challenging experiences, providing interpretive frameworks connecting specific situations with underlying principles and developmental opportunities.

**Personalized Adaptation:** Mentors help trainees identify individual expression of traditional principles appropriate to personal temperament, allowing authentic commitment without forced conformity to single manifestation pattern.

**Corrective Accountability:** Established oath-bearers maintain tradition integrity by addressing principle misapplications, challenging rationalized compromises, and guiding aspirants through failure recovery without principle abandonment.

**Ceremonial Witnessing:** Mentors typically play formal role within oath ceremonies, serving as official witnesses creating accountability relationship extending beyond training period into active service.
