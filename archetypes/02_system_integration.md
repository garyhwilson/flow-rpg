# Archetype System Integration

This document explains how Archetypes integrate with the core Flow RPG system mechanics, detailing how these professional traditions interact with skills, combat, magic, and other subsystems.

## Archetype and Skill System

### Skill Proficiency Framework

Archetypes determine a character's starting skill proficiencies, providing a foundation of professional training:

- **Professional (+2):** Core competencies central to the archetype's function
- **Competent (+0):** Secondary skills that support the archetype's approach
- **Basic (not specified):** Peripheral skills with casual familiarity
- **Untrained (-2):** Skills outside the archetype's typical training

### Skill Advancement Patterns

While any character can advance any skill, each archetype offers natural advancement paths:

- **Primary Path:** Skills central to archetype identity advance at standard cost
- **Secondary Path:** Related skills advance at standard cost
- **Cross-Training:** Skills from other archetypes advance at increased cost

### Archetype Skill Specialties

Each archetype approaches skills with distinctive focus areas:

**Fighter:**
- **Combat:** Weapon specialization and tactical positioning
- **Athletics:** Endurance and strength application
- **Command:** Battlefield leadership and intimidation

**Ranger:**
- **Combat:** Ranged combat and mobility-based attacks
- **Survival:** Tracking, foraging, and navigation
- **Investigate:** Environmental awareness and clue detection

**Paladin:**
- **Combat:** Defensive techniques and protective positioning
- **Medicine:** Battlefield first aid and condition treatment
- **Command:** Inspirational leadership and presence

**Rogue:**
- **Stealth:** Concealment and silent movement
- **Finesse:** Lock manipulation and sleight of hand
- **Deceive:** Misdirection and false personas

**Wizard:**
- **Sorcery:** Analytical spellcasting and arcane theory
- **Lore:** Academic knowledge and research methodology
- **Investigate:** Pattern recognition and magical analysis

**Cleric:**
- **Medicine:** Comprehensive healing and disease treatment
- **Sorcery:** Faith-based magical practice
- **Empathy:** Spiritual counseling and emotional support

**Bard:**
- **Perform:** Artistic expression and audience influence
- **Deceive:** Storytelling and persona adoption
- **Sorcery:** Performance-based magical effects

**Ranger:**
- **Preferred Stance:** Aggressive (mobility and offense)
- **Secondary Stance:** Balanced (adaptability)
- **Stance Switching:** Environmental response and target assessment

**Paladin:**
- **Preferred Stance:** Defensive (protection focus)
- **Secondary Stance:** Balanced (leadership position)
- **Stance Switching:** Threat response and ally protection

**Rogue:**
- **Preferred Stance:** Aggressive (advantage exploitation)
- **Secondary Stance:** Balanced (positioning setup)
- **Stance Switching:** Opportunity-based transitions

**Wizard:**
- **Preferred Stance:** Balanced (analytical assessment)
- **Secondary Stance:** Defensive (safety priority)
- **Stance Switching:** Spell requirement adaptation

**Cleric:**
- **Preferred Stance:** Defensive (ally protection)
- **Secondary Stance:** Balanced (support positioning)

**Bard:**
- **Preferred Stance:** Balanced (performance platform)
- **Secondary Stance:** Varies (narrative appropriate)
- **Stance Switching:** Audience/ally response adaptation

### Combat Zone Positioning

Archetypes have characteristic zone positioning strategies:

**Fighter:**
- **Preferred Zone:** Engaged with primary threats
- **Zone Control:** Maintains position against forced movement
- **Zone Movement:** Tactical repositioning for advantage

**Ranger:**
- **Preferred Zone:** Far (for ranged) or mobile between zones
- **Zone Control:** Environmental advantage utilization
- **Zone Movement:** Efficient traversal of difficult terrain

**Paladin:**
- **Preferred Zone:** Between allies and threats
- **Zone Control:** Presence that discourages enemy movement
- **Zone Movement:** Intercept positioning to protect allies

**Rogue:**
- **Preferred Zone:** Advantageous position regardless of distance
- **Zone Control:** Opportunity creation through positioning
- **Zone Movement:** Unimpeded traverse using environmental features

**Wizard:**
- **Preferred Zone:** Far (safety with visibility)
- **Zone Control:** Area denial through magical effects
- **Zone Movement:** Tactical withdrawal and strategic positioning

**Cleric:**
- **Preferred Zone:** Close to allies needing support
- **Zone Control:** Protective presence that deters threats
- **Zone Movement:** Response positioning to provide aid

**Bard:**
- **Preferred Zone:** Position of maximum ally influence
- **Zone Control:** Performance space establishing
- **Zone Movement:** Audience/ally engagement optimization

### Weapon and Armor Preferences

Equipment specialization varies by archetype:

**Fighter:**
- **Weapons:** Versatile martial weapons with reliable damage
- **Armor:** Medium to heavy, balancing protection and mobility
- **Equipment Focus:** Combat effectiveness and durability

**Ranger:**
- **Weapons:** Ranged primary with versatile melee backup
- **Armor:** Light to medium, prioritizing mobility
- **Equipment Focus:** Adaptability and environmental utility

**Paladin:**
- **Weapons:** Symbolic and effective melee weapons
- **Armor:** Heavy, maximizing protective capability
- **Equipment Focus:** Durability and symbolic representation

**Rogue:**
- **Weapons:** Light, concealable, or dual-wielded
- **Armor:** Light or none, prioritizing mobility and stealth
- **Equipment Focus:** Utility and specialized tools

**Wizard:**
- **Weapons:** Staff, dagger, or other arcane implements
- **Armor:** None or light, avoiding spell interference
- **Equipment Focus:** Magical components and knowledge tools

**Cleric:**
- **Weapons:** Symbol-appropriate and defensive designs
- **Armor:** Medium, balancing protection with casting ability
- **Equipment Focus:** Healing supplies and faith symbols

**Bard:**
- **Weapons:** Elegant, performance-compatible designs
- **Armor:** Light, aesthetically integrated with performance
- **Equipment Focus:** Instruments, performance tools, and social utility

### Archetype Interactions

How archetypes typically relate to each other:

**Natural Allies:**
- Fighter & Cleric: Protection and support synergy
- Wizard & Rogue: Knowledge and application complementarity
- Ranger & Druid: Environmental focus alignment
- Paladin & Bard: Inspiration and leadership complementarity

**Potential Tensions:**
- Fighter & Wizard: Physical vs. magical approach
- Cleric & Rogue: Moral structure vs. opportunistic flexibility
- Paladin & Ranger: Civilization vs. wilderness priority
- Bard & Scholar: Emotional vs. analytical knowledge approach

**Productive Partnerships:**
- Fighter provides protection for Wizard's concentration
- Ranger scouts ahead while Rogue infiltrates identified targets
- Cleric supports Paladin's frontline presence
- Bard enhances entire party with inspirational performance
