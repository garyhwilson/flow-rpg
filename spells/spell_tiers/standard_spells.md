# TIER 1: BASIC SPELLS (0 MOMENTUM)

## Overview

Tier 1 spells represent the fundamental magical effects that all casters rely on. Costing 0 Momentum, these spells maintain Momentum equilibriumâ€”making them sustainable and reliable for regular use. They provide significantly more power than cantrips while remaining accessible even at low Momentum. A well-chosen basic spell at the right moment can turn the tide of an encounter without depleting your resources.

## Design Philosophy

Basic spells occupy the foundational tier in the magic system:

1. **Sustainable Use** - No Momentum cost means unlimited casting potential
2. **Meaningful Impact** - Significantly more powerful than cantrips
3. **Tactical Variety** - Different spells for different situations
4. **Momentum Neutral** - Maintain your Momentum state while producing powerful effects
5. **Combat Staple** - The bread and butter of magical combat

Experienced casters use basic spells as their primary magical tool, building Momentum with cantrips and spending it on advanced/master spells for critical moments.

## Casting Basic Spells

**Casting Roll:** 2d6 + [Chosen Attribute] + Sorcery
**Momentum Cost:** 0 Momentum (no Momentum cost to cast)

- Choose which attribute to use (Mind, Awareness, Will, or Presence)
- Each attribute provides different benefits
- Can cast at any Momentum level (even negative Momentum)
- Still affected by armor penalties
- Combine with Aggressive stance to generate +1 Momentum on damage

**Success Thresholds:**

- **6 or less:** Failure (generates +1 Momentum for enemy)
- **7-9:** Partial success (reduced effect or complication)
- **10+:** Full success (described effect occurs)
- **13+:** Critical success (full effect plus +1 Momentum)

## BOLT

**Ranged magical attack at Far range**

Bolt launches a concentrated projectile of magical energy at a distant target. It's the upgrade from Spark, trading Momentum cost for extended range and increased damage. Every combat caster relies on Bolt for ranged offense.

### Mind + Sorcery: Calculated Trajectory

- **Damage:** Mind + d6
- **Range:** Far
- **Special:** Ignores cover (calculated perfect trajectory around obstacles)
- **Narrative:** Impossible angles, ricochets around corners, mathematical precision
- **Tactical Use:** Hitting enemies behind cover, avoiding line-of-sight issues

### Awareness + Sorcery: Seeking Strike

- **Damage:** Awareness + d6
- **Range:** Far
- **Special:** Seeks weak points (maximum damage potential)
- **Narrative:** Instinctive aim, senses vulnerabilities, unerring accuracy
- **Tactical Use:** Pure damage dealing, exploiting weaknesses

### Will + Sorcery: Unwavering Blast

- **Damage:** Will + d6
- **Range:** Far
- **Special:** Can cast at negative Momentum
- **Narrative:** Powered by determination, channeled through force of will, never fails
- **Tactical Use:** When depleted but need ranged attack

### Presence + Sorcery: Terror Bolt

- **Damage:** Presence + d6
- **Range:** Far
- **Special:** Spectacular display causes fear (targets near victim hesitate)
- **Narrative:** Explosive impact, terrifying power, dramatic effect
- **Tactical Use:** Damage + battlefield control through intimidation

### GM Notes

- Bolt is the standard ranged attack for casters
- Mind's cover-ignoring is significant in tactical combat
- Awareness typically does most damage (highest attribute)
- Presence fear effect is narrative but can affect enemy tactics
- Far range means casters can stay safe

## SHIELD

**Protective ward for self or ally**

Shield creates a magical barrier around the caster or an ally, providing sustained protection throughout a scene. Unlike Mage Armor, Shield can protect others and scales with your casting attribute.

### Mind + Sorcery: Calculated Defense

- **Effect:** +Mind to Guard for scene
- **Range:** Near
- **Special:** Can shield two adjacent targets (within Near of each other) for the same -1 Momentum cost through optimized energy distribution
- **Narrative:** Geometric force fields, mathematical deflection patterns, shared protective matrix
- **Tactical Use:** Efficiently protect multiple allies simultaneously

### Awareness + Sorcery: Precognitive Ward

- **Effect:** +Awareness to Guard for scene
- **Range:** Near
- **Special:** Provides warning of attacks (narrative advantage)
- **Narrative:** Instinctive defense, sense attacks before they land, reactive barrier
- **Tactical Use:** Protecting allies who rely on avoiding hits

### Will + Sorcery: Enduring Aegis

- **Effect:** +Will to Guard for scene
- **Range:** Near
- **Special:** Maintains even when caster unconscious
- **Narrative:** Powered by determination, refuses to fail, unshakable
- **Tactical Use:** Reliable protection that doesn't depend on concentration

### Presence + Sorcery: Demoralizing Barrier

- **Effect:** +Presence to Guard for scene
- **Range:** Near
- **Special:** Impressive barrier demoralizes attackers (enemies hesitate)
- **Narrative:** Visibly powerful protection, displays magical prowess, intimidating
- **Tactical Use:** Protection + psychological warfare

### Important Rules

- **Stacks with Armor:** Unlike Mage Armor, Shield is additive
- **Lasts for Scene:** Continues until scene ends
- **Can Target Others:** Near range allows protecting allies
- **No Concentration:** Persists through stance changes and other actions

### GM Notes

- Shield is incredibly efficient protection
- +3 Guard for scene at -1 Momentum is excellent value
- Will version continuing after unconsciousness is powerful
- Mind version can protect two adjacent allies for -1 Momentum total (exceptional value)
- Other versions can cast on multiple allies (costs 1 Momentum each)
- Combine with Mage Armor for layered defense

## CHARM

**Influence target's mind**

Charm magically influences a target's thoughts and feelings. It's the social caster's most versatile tool, adapting to different approaches from subtle suggestion to outright domination. The effects depend heavily on the casting attribute.

### Mind + Sorcery: Logical Persuasion

- **Effect:** Target's actions must be "logical" from their perspective
- **Range:** Near
- **Special:** +Mind to Social rolls involving charmed target
- **Narrative:** Plant logical-seeming ideas, make suggestions appear reasonable
- **Duration:** Scene or until you do something illogical
- **Tactical Use:** Subtle manipulation, negotiation, avoiding combat

### Awareness + Sorcery: Empathic Bond

- **Effect:** Sense target's desires and fears
- **Range:** Near
- **Special:** +Awareness to Social rolls, know what they want
- **Narrative:** Feel their emotions, understand their motivations instinctively
- **Duration:** Scene
- **Tactical Use:** Investigation, negotiation with perfect information

### Will + Sorcery: Dominating Command

- **Effect:** If target's Will is lower than yours, they must obey
- **Range:** Near
- **Special:** Absolute control over weaker-willed targets
- **Narrative:** Force of will overrides their desires, command compliance
- **Duration:** Scene or until you command something against their nature
- **Tactical Use:** Controlling weak-willed enemies, simple commands

### Presence + Sorcery: Irresistible Charisma

- **Effect:** +Presence to Social rolls
- **Range:** Near
- **Special:** Target wants to help you, sees you as fascinating
- **Narrative:** Become magnetically charismatic, irresistibly appealing
- **Duration:** Scene
- **Tactical Use:** Social encounters, gathering allies, avoiding combat

### Important Rules

- **Social Spell:** Doesn't work mid-combat usually
- **Awareness Required:** Target must be aware of you
- **GM Adjudication:** Exact effects depend on approach and situation
- **Not Mind Control:** Even Will version has limits (won't jump off cliff)

### GM Notes

- Charm is powerful but requires careful adjudication
- Will version is closest to true mind control
- Mind and Presence are subtler, more social
- Awareness version is information-gathering
- Can't make targets do things fundamentally against their nature
- May break if you attack charmed target

## BLUR

**Defensive obscurement, self only**

Blur makes you harder to hit by distorting your appearance. Whether through calculated probability manipulation, instinctive dodging, unshakable determination, or mesmerizing movements, Blur provides excellent defense for a modest Momentum cost.

### Mind + Sorcery: Probability Distortion

- **Effect:** +2 to defense rolls
- **Range:** Self
- **Special:** Calculated probability manipulation (attacks miss by impossible coincidence)
- **Narrative:** Statistical improbability, calculated evasion, perfect timing
- **Duration:** Scene

### Awareness + Sorcery: Precognitive Dodge

- **Effect:** +2 to defense rolls
- **Range:** Self
- **Special:** Instinctively dodge before attacks land (start moving before enemy swings)
- **Narrative:** See attacks coming, sense danger, reactive instinct
- **Duration:** Scene

### Will + Sorcery: Unshakable Form

- **Effect:** +2 to defense rolls
- **Range:** Self
- **Special:** Maintain even when stunned, dazed, or disrupted
- **Narrative:** Refuse to be hit, determination manifests as protection
- **Duration:** Scene

### Presence + Sorcery: Mesmerizing Motion

- **Effect:** +2 to defense rolls
- **Range:** Self
- **Special:** Distracting movements confuse attackers (they aim wrong)
- **Narrative:** Hypnotic movements, theatrical flourishes, captivating presence
- **Duration:** Scene

### Important Rules

- **Self Only:** Cannot cast on allies (use Shield for that)
- **Stacks with Guard:** +2 is additional defense
- **Lasts Scene:** Very efficient protection
- **Works with Mage Armor/Shield:** Can layer defenses

### GM Notes

- Blur + Shield + Mage Armor = very well-protected caster
- +2 defense is significant (approximately +33% survival)
- Will version working through disruption is unique advantage
- All versions are mechanically similar (choose for narrative)
- Different descriptions for each attribute make same bonus feel different

## Standard Spell Tactics

### Spell Selection

**Combat Scenarios:**

- **Pure Offense:** Bolt (ranged damage)
- **Team Protection:** Shield (defend allies)
- **Self-Preservation:** Blur (personal defense)
- **Control:** Charm (remove threats socially)

**Social Scenarios:**

- **Negotiation:** Charm (all versions)
- **Investigation:** Charm with Awareness (sense motives)
- **Intimidation:** Bolt for display (show power)
- **Impressing:** Shield with Presence (show prowess)

### Momentum Efficiency

**At 1-2 Momentum:**

- One standard spell, then cantrips
- Choose spell carefully for maximum impact
- Consider Defensive Stance to rebuild Momentum

**At 3-4 Momentum:**

- Can cast multiple standard spells
- Mix offense and defense
- Save some Momentum for advanced spell if needed

**At 5+ Momentum:**

- Standard spells are cheap
- Use freely for optimal tactics
- Save Momentum for master spell finisher

### Attribute Selection

**When to Use Each Attribute:**

**Mind:**

- Need precision (Bolt ignoring cover)
- Want reliability (calculated effects)
- Logical persuasion (Charm)
- Planning defense (Shield angles)

**Awareness:**

- Want maximum damage (Bolt seeks weaknesses)
- Need information (Charm senses desires)
- Reactive defense (Blur dodges)
- Instinctive protection (Shield warns)

**Will:**

- At low/negative Momentum (can still cast)
- Need reliability (Shield persists)
- Want absolute effect (Charm dominates)
- Maintain through disruption (Blur unshakable)

**Presence:**

- Want psychological impact (Bolt terrifies)
- Social situations (Charm charisma)
- Showing off (Shield impressive)
- Distraction (Blur mesmerizing)

## Standard Spell Combinations

### Combat Combos

**Shield + Aggressive Stance:**

1. Cast Shield on ally for -1 Momentum
2. Enter Aggressive Stance
3. Ally can fight safely with bonus Guard
4. You generate Momentum from offensive actions

**Blur + Bolt Spam:**

1. Cast Blur for personal defense (-1 Momentum)
2. Spend remaining turns using Bolt
3. Stay at range, well-defended
4. Efficient damage dealing

**Double Shield:**

1. Shield on frontline fighter (-1 Momentum)
2. Shield on self (-1 Momentum)
3. Both protected for whole scene
4. Great Momentum investment

### Social Combos

**Charm + Conversation:**

1. Cast Charm at start of scene (-1 Momentum)
2. Bonus applies to all Social rolls
3. Scene-long advantage in negotiation
4. Different attributes for different approaches

**Blur + Escape:**

1. Cast Blur for defense (-1 Momentum)
2. Attempt to flee
3. Harder to hit during escape
4. Covers tactical withdrawal

### Defensive Combos

**The Bunker:**

1. Cast Mage Armor (0 Momentum)
2. Cast Shield on self (-1 Momentum)
3. Cast Blur (-1 Momentum)
4. Nearly unhittable caster

## Custom Standard Spells

GMs may allow custom standard spells following these guidelines:

**Power Level:**

- **Damage:** Attribute + d6, single target or small area
- **Defense:** +2-3 bonus for scene or +Attribute bonus for scene
- **Duration:** Scene maximum, instant for damage
- **Range:** Near to Far
- **Targets:** Self, single target, or small group
- **Momentum Cost:** -1 Momentum

**Design Template:**

**[Spell Name] - [Basic Description]**

- **Mind:** [Precision/control/calculation benefit]
- **Awareness:** [Instinct/sensing/maximum benefit]
- **Will:** [Reliability/persistence/force benefit]
- **Presence:** [Social/dramatic/fear benefit]

**Example Custom Standard Spell: Spectral Weapon**

- **Mind:** Create weapon, +Mind to attack for scene (precise strikes)
- **Awareness:** Create weapon, +Awareness to attack for scene (instinctive combat)
- **Will:** Create weapon, +Will to attack for scene (never drops, works at negative Momentum)
- **Presence:** Create weapon, +Presence to attack for scene (terrifying appearance)

## Narrative Uses for Standard Spells

Beyond mechanical combat applications:

**Bolt:**

- Breaking objects at range
- Signaling across distances
- Demonstrating power
- Lighting fires/destroying evidence

**Shield:**

- Protecting from environmental hazards
- Shielding against non-combat dangers (falling rocks)
- Showing care for allies
- Displaying magical ability

**Charm:**

- Gathering information
- Making allies
- Avoiding combat entirely
- Building relationships

**Blur:**

- Sneaking past guards (harder to see clearly)
- Performing tricks (appears supernatural)
- Escaping pursuit
- Looking mysterious

## Standard Spell Mastery

Skilled casters use standard spells efficiently:

**Early Combat:**

- Establish defenses (Shield, Blur)
- Position for advantage
- Build Momentum while protected

**Mid Combat:**

- Bolt for consistent damage
- Maintain defensive spells
- Generate Momentum for bigger spells

**Late Combat:**

- Finish with advanced/master spells
- Or continue Bolt efficiency
- Standard spells remain viable throughout

**Social Encounters:**

- Open with Charm
- Leverage bonus throughout scene
- Solve problems without violence

_Standard spells represent the core of practical magic. Master them, and you master the art of efficient spellcasting._
