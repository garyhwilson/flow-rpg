# CHARACTER CREATION

Creating a character involves a series of meaningful choices that define who your character is, what they can do, and why they adventure.

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Choose Your Attributes
Select your starting attribute array and assign values to the six attributes.

### Step 2: Choose Your Species
Select one of the four species, applying your attribute bonus and gaining species traits.

### Step 3: Choose Your Archetype
Pick one of seven archetypes that determines your starting skills and equipment.

### Step 4: Choose Your Calling
Select one of nine callings that defines your inner motivation and provides special abilities.

### Step 5: Calculate Derived Values
Determine your Guard, Vitality, and other derived statistics.

### Step 6: Add Final Details
Name your character, develop a brief background, and create connections.

---

## Attributes

Attributes range from -5 to +5, with 0 representing the human average.

### Starting Arrays (choose one):
- **Standard:** +2, +1, +1, 0, 0, -1
- **Specialized:** +3, +2, 0, 0, -1, -2
- **Balanced:** +1, +1, +1, 0, 0, 0

### The Six Attributes:
- **Might** - Physical force, strength, and endurance
- **Grace** - Precision, agility, and coordination
- **Mind** - Reasoning, intellect, and knowledge
- **Awareness** - Perception, intuition, and attunement
- **Will** - Fortitude, determination, and spiritual power
- **Presence** - Charisma, magnetism, and social influence

## Species

Your species provides an attribute bonus and unique traits that integrate with the Flow system.

### DWARF
*Sturdy mountain folk known for resilience and craftsmanship*

**Attribute Bonus:** +1 Might or +1 Will (your choice)

**Species Traits:**
- **Stone Endurance:** When your Guard is depleted, reduce the first Vitality damage each scene by 2 (innate toughness)
- **Craftwise:** When examining worked materials (stone, metal, crafts), ask one question the GM must answer truthfully
- **Steady Stance:** Switching from Defensive stance costs no Flow (stubborn immovability)

**Common Callings:** Sentinel, Guardian, Advocate
**Natural Archetypes:** Fighter, Paladin, Cleric

### ELF
*Graceful people attuned to magic and nature*

**Attribute Bonus:** +1 Grace or +1 Awareness (your choice)

**Species Traits:**
- **Fey Grace:** When you gain Flow from critical success (13+), gain +1 additional Flow
- **Magical Affinity:** You can sense magic without the Sorcery skill (but can't identify details without it)
- **Elven Focus:** When casting spells, ignore the first point of armor penalty

**Common Callings:** Scholar, Seeker, Mediator
**Natural Archetypes:** Wizard, Ranger, Bard

### HALFLING
*Small, nimble folk with remarkable luck and courage*

**Attribute Bonus:** +1 Will or +1 Presence (your choice)

**Species Traits:**
- **Lucky:** Once per scene, when your Flow would go negative, it stops at 0 instead
- **Brave Heart:** When an ally goes to negative Flow, you immediately gain +1 Flow
- **Small but Mighty:** You can move through enemy zones without provoking, but your melee weapon damage is -1

**Common Callings:** Enthusiast, Guardian, Mediator
**Natural Archetypes:** Rogue, Bard, Cleric

### HUMAN
*Versatile and ambitious, adapting to any challenge*

**Attribute Bonus:** +1 to any attribute of your choice

**Species Traits:**
- **Versatile:** Start with one extra Competent skill OR upgrade one starting Competent to Professional
- **Determined:** When at negative Flow, your next successful action gains +1 additional Flow
- **Adaptable:** During any rest, you may swap one skill at Competent level with another

**Common Callings:** Any - humans show no pattern
**Natural Archetypes:** Any - human flexibility

## Archetypes

Your archetype determines your initial skill training, equipment, and combat role.

### FIGHTER
*Master of weapons and combat techniques*

**Key Skills:**
- Combat (Professional +2)
- Athletics (Competent +0)
- Command (Competent +0)

**Starting Equipment:** Medium armor, martial weapon, shield
**Natural Callings:** Champion, Guardian, Sentinel

### RANGER
*Wilderness expert and skilled hunter*

**Key Skills:**
- Combat (Competent +0)
- Survival (Professional +2)
- Investigate (Competent +0)

**Starting Equipment:** Light armor, bow, tracking kit
**Natural Callings:** Seeker, Scholar, Sentinel

### PALADIN
*Divine warrior and inspiring leader*

**Key Skills:**
- Combat (Competent +0)
- Medicine (Competent +0)
- Command (Professional +2)

**Starting Equipment:** Heavy armor, weapon, holy symbol
**Natural Callings:** Advocate, Guardian, Champion

### ROGUE
*Skilled infiltrator and versatile expert*

**Key Skills:**
- Stealth (Professional +2)
- Finesse (Professional +2)
- Deceive (Competent +0)

**Starting Equipment:** Light armor, daggers, thieves' tools
**Natural Callings:** Seeker, Enthusiast, Scholar

### WIZARD
*Master of arcane knowledge and magical theory*

**Key Skills:**
- Sorcery (Professional +2)
- Lore (Professional +2)
- Investigate (Competent +0)

**Starting Equipment:** Robes, spellbook, component pouch
**Natural Callings:** Scholar, Seeker, Advocate
**Special:** Can cast spells if Mind + Awareness ≥ +2

### CLERIC
*Channel of divine power and spiritual healer*

**Key Skills:**
- Medicine (Professional +2)
- Sorcery (Competent +0)
- Empathy (Professional +2)

**Starting Equipment:** Medium armor, mace, holy symbol
**Natural Callings:** Guardian, Mediator, Advocate
**Special:** Can cast spells if Mind + Awareness ≥ +2

### BARD
*Performer, storyteller, and magical artist*

**Key Skills:**
- Perform (Professional +2)
- Deceive (Competent +0)
- Sorcery (Competent +0)

**Starting Equipment:** Light armor, instrument, rapier
**Natural Callings:** Enthusiast, Mediator, Seeker
**Special:** Can cast spells if Mind + Awareness ≥ +2

*Note: All other skills start at Untrained (-2)*

## Callings

Your Calling represents your inner drive and provides Flow benefits and narrative permissions.

### 1. THE ADVOCATE
*"The world must be made right"*

**Core Drive:** To improve, perfect, and bring order to chaos
**Fear:** Being corrupt, defective, or wrong
**Desire:** To be good, right, accurate, and virtuous

**Flow Benefit - Righteous Fury:** When you expose corruption, lies, or injustice through your actions, immediately gain 1 Flow (once per scene)

**Narrative Permission - Truth Sense:** You can always tell when someone is lying about wrongdoing or corruption. No roll needed - you simply know.

### 2. THE GUARDIAN
*"I exist to help others"*

**Core Drive:** To feel needed and appreciated through service
**Fear:** Being unloved or unwanted
**Desire:** To feel loved and needed by others

**Flow Benefit - Protective Surge:** When an ally within Close range takes damage that would reduce them to 0 Vitality, you may immediately spend 1 Flow to reduce that damage by half (once per scene)

**Narrative Permission - Helper's Intuition:** You always know when someone genuinely needs help, even if they're trying to hide it. No roll needed.

### 3. THE EXEMPLAR
*"Success proves my worth"*

**Core Drive:** To achieve, excel, and be valued for accomplishments
**Fear:** Being worthless or without value apart from achievements
**Desire:** To feel valuable and worthwhile

**Flow Benefit - Victory Rush:** When you achieve a Critical Success (13+) on a declared "signature" action, gain 1 Flow and your next roll gains +1d6 (once per session, must declare signature action at scene start)

**Narrative Permission - Victory Sense:** You can instinctively assess the "win condition" of any situation - what would constitute success here. No roll needed.

### 4. THE SEEKER
*"I must find my true self"*

**Core Drive:** To understand themselves and find their unique identity
**Fear:** Having no identity or significance
**Desire:** To find themselves and their significance

**Flow Benefit - Authentic Insight:** Once per scene, when you act against conventional wisdom or expectations, gain 1 Flow. Additionally, you may reroll any one die that shows a 1, keeping the new result.

**Narrative Permission - Soul Reading:** Once per scene, you can ask one creature a deep personal question that they must answer honestly (though they may be cryptic).

### 5. THE SCHOLAR
*"Knowledge reveals truth"*

**Core Drive:** To understand, comprehend, and master knowledge
**Fear:** Being incompetent, invaded, or overwhelmed
**Desire:** To be capable and understanding

**Flow Benefit - Prepared Mind:** Once per scene, after seeing the result of any roll (yours or another's), you may declare you predicted this outcome. The roll gains +1d6 retroactively. If this changes a failure to success, gain 1 Flow.

**Narrative Permission - Contingency Planning:** Once per scene, declare you prepared for this exact situation and produce a relevant tool, fact, or minor preparation.

### 6. THE SENTINEL
*"Loyalty and vigilance keep us safe"*

**Core Drive:** To find security and support through loyalty and preparation
**Fear:** Being without support, guidance, or security
**Desire:** To have security and support

**Flow Benefit - Defensive Vigilance:** When you take the Defensive stance, you may protect one Close ally with your Guard value (they use your Guard instead of theirs). Once per scene, when doing so prevents damage, gain 1 Flow.

**Narrative Permission - Danger Sense:** You cannot be surprised or ambushed. You always act in the first round of combat, even if caught off-guard.

### 7. THE ENTHUSIAST
*"Life is an adventure to experience"*

**Core Drive:** To maintain happiness, freedom, and avoid pain
**Fear:** Being trapped in pain or deprivation
**Desire:** To be satisfied and content, to have needs fulfilled

**Flow Benefit - Joyful Flow:** When you gain Flow from any source, you may immediately gift it to an adjacent ally instead of keeping it (no action required).

**Narrative Permission - Silver Lining:** You can always find something positive or useful in any situation, no matter how dire. This insight provides actual mechanical or narrative benefit.

### 8. THE CHAMPION
*"Strength protects what matters"*

**Core Drive:** To be self-reliant, in control, and protect others
**Fear:** Being controlled, vulnerable, or weak
**Desire:** To be self-reliant and in control of their destiny

**Flow Benefit - Protective Dominance:** When you deal damage to an enemy that just harmed an ally, that damage cannot be reduced by Guard. Once per scene when you do this, gain 1 Flow.

**Narrative Permission - Unshakeable Will:** Intimidation attempts against you automatically fail. You project strength that cannot be undermined.

### 9. THE MEDIATOR
*"Harmony brings peace to all"*

**Core Drive:** To maintain inner and outer peace and harmony
**Fear:** Loss of connection, separation, conflict
**Desire:** To have inner and outer peace

**Flow Benefit - Harmony Restoration:** Once per scene, when you successfully defuse a conflict without violence (through roleplay and appropriate skill checks), you and all participants gain 1 Flow.

**Narrative Permission - Universal Understanding:** You understand the basic meaning and emotional content of any communication, regardless of language barriers.

## Derived Values

### Health
- **Guard:** 5 + Might + Milestones Achieved
  - Ablative defense that absorbs damage first
  - Recoverable between scenes
  - Can be restored in combat with Rally action
- **Vitality:** 10 + Will
  - Core health representing serious injury
  - Damage to Vitality causes lasting consequences
  - At 0 Vitality: Unconscious and dying

### Flow
- **Starting Flow:** 0
- **Range:** -6 to +10
- **Flow Gates:** Some abilities require +3 or +6 Flow to activate

### Magic Access
- **Can Cast Spells:** Only if Mind + Awareness ≥ +2
- **Note:** Wizards, Clerics, and Bards still need this requirement!

### Movement & Zones
- **Speed:** One zone per round (standard)
- **Engaged:** Melee combat distance
- **Close:** One move away (throwing range)
- **Far:** Two moves away (bow range)

## Character Sheet Organization

### Front Page
- **Character Concept:** Name, species, archetype, calling
- **Attributes:** Six core attributes with modifiers
- **Skills:** All fifteen skills with current ratings
- **Derived Values:** Guard, Vitality, Flow
- **Combat Stats:** Stance preferences, weapons, armor

### Back Page
- **Species Traits:** Your three species abilities
- **Archetype Abilities:** Skills and special features
- **Calling Benefits:** Flow benefit and narrative permission
- **Equipment:** Gear, wealth, and possessions
- **Background:** History, connections, and goals

## Character Creation Example

**Thornwick Ironfoot**
- **Species:** Dwarf (+1 Will chosen)
- **Attributes:** Specialized Array: Might +2, Grace -2, Mind -1, Awareness 0, Will +3[+1], Presence 0
- **Archetype:** Cleric
  - Medicine (Professional +2)
  - Sorcery (Competent +0)
  - Empathy (Professional +2)
- **Calling:** The Guardian
  - Can spend 1 Flow to halve damage to dying ally
  - Always knows who needs help
- **Derived Values:**
  - Guard: 7 (5 + Might 2)
  - Vitality: 14 (10 + Will 4)
  - Magic Access: No (Mind -1 + Awareness 0 = -1, needs +2)
  - *Note: This Cleric cannot cast spells! Must rely on Medicine and Empathy*

Thornwick is a non-magical healer who protects through skill and determination rather than divine magic. His Guardian calling makes him exceptionally attuned to others' needs, while his dwarven resilience allows him to endure hardship to help others.

- Support-focused

- Small crusader for justice

**"The Eternal Student"**
