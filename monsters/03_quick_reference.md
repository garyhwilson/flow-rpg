## FLOW REFERENCE

### Players Gain Flow

- **Defeating any adversary:** +1 Flow
- **Defeating major villain:** +2 Flow (GM discretion)
- **Successful attack in Aggressive Stance:** +1 Flow

### Adversaries Gain Flow

- Same rules as players (if intelligent)
- Mindless creatures don't track Flow
- Most minor opponents don't manage Flow tactically

## POWERFUL ADVERSARY RULES

### Multiple Actions

Major adversaries may act more than once per round.

**Critical rule:** Limit to 2 ATTACKS per round maximum

Third action options: Movement, abilities, defensive stance, environment interaction

### Legendary Resistance

Major villains can shrug off 2-3 effects per fight

Use sparingly - players should feel their abilities matter

### Lair Advantages

In their territory, powerful adversaries may have:
- Environmental hazards they're immune to
- Arriving reinforcements
- Magical effects woven into the location

## BEHAVIOR BY INTELLIGENCE

### Mindless (zombies, constructs, oozes)
- Attack nearest target
- No tactics, no retreat
- Fight until destroyed
- Predictable patterns

### Animal (wolves, bears, giant spiders)
- Pack tactics if social
- Target weak/wounded
- Flee when badly hurt
- Defend territory

### Average (bandits, soldiers, cultists)
- Coordinate with allies
- Use cover and terrain
- Retreat strategically
- Can be negotiated with

### High (dragons, archmages, masterminds)
- Complex tactics
- Prepared contingencies
- Exploit weaknesses
- Strategic retreats

## IMPROVISING ADVERSARIES

### 30-Second Creation

1. **Choose capability level** (inexperienced/trained/veteran/legendary)
2. **Assign Guard and Vitality** from table above
3. **Set combat modifier** (+1 to +5)
4. **Pick one ability** that fits their concept
5. **Decide their personality** (aggressive, cautious, protective, cowardly)

### Example

**Need:** A corrupt merchant's bodyguard

**Statistics:** Trained level - Guard 16, Vitality 18, Combat +3

**Ability:** "Protective Stance" - When the merchant is attacked, can intercept (1 Flow)

**Personality:** Professional, loyal to whoever's paying, will surrender if merchant flees

Natural animals, instinctive behavior, no tools

## COMBAT CHECKLIST

### Before Combat
- [ ] Name the significant adversaries
- [ ] Know what they want
- [ ] Note environmental features
- [ ] Assign starting Flow

### During Combat
- [ ] Give adversaries personality
- [ ] Track Vitality and Flow
- [ ] Have adversaries retreat when sensible
- [ ] Keep things moving (4-6 rounds ideal)

### After Combat
- [ ] Award Flow for defeats
- [ ] Handle prisoners/wounded
- [ ] Note who escaped
- [ ] Consider consequences

## WHEN THINGS GO WRONG

### Fight Too Easy
- Reinforcements arrive
- Environmental complication
- Adversary reveals hidden ability

### Fight Too Hard
- Adversaries make tactical errors
- Morale breaks, some flee
- Environmental opportunity for players

### Combat Dragging
- Adversaries make decisive moves
- Narrate conclusion if outcome obvious
- "With their leader down, the rest surrender"
