## ENCOUNTER BUILDING BY PARTY SIZE

### 4-Player Party (Standard)

**Easy Encounter:**
- 4-6 Threat 1 (minions)
- 2 Threat 2 (standard)
- 1 Threat 2 + 2-3 Threat 1

**Moderate Encounter:**
- 3-4 Threat 2 (standard)
- 1 Threat 3 (elite) + 2-4 Threat 1
- 2 Threat 3 (elite)

**Hard Encounter:**
- 1 Threat 3 + 2-3 Threat 2
- 2 Threat 3 + 2-4 Threat 1
- 1 Threat 4 (legendary)

**Epic Encounter:**
- 1 Threat 4 + 2-4 Threat 2
- 1 Threat 4 + 1 Threat 3 + 2-4 Threat 1

### Scaling for Other Party Sizes

**3-Player Party:**
Reduce enemy count by 25% or lower threat level by 1

**5-Player Party:**
Increase enemy count by 25% or add +1 Threat 2 enemy

**6-Player Party:**
Increase enemy count by 50% or add +1 Threat 3 enemy

## FLOW GENERATION QUICK REFERENCE

### Player Flow Gain
- **Successful attack** in Aggressive Stance: +1 Flow
- **Defeat enemy:** +1 Flow (any enemy Threat 1-3)
- **Defeat legendary adversary:** +2 Flow (Threat 4)
- **Milestone achievement:** +1-3 Flow (GM discretion)

### Monster Flow Gain
- **Successful attack:** +1 Flow (if intelligent/tactical)
- **Starts combat:** Flow listed in stat block
- **Never generates from defeating PCs** (would create death spiral)

## COMMON MONSTER ABILITIES BY COST

### 0 Flow (Natural Abilities)
- Breath weapons (recharge mechanic)
- Passive resistances/immunities
- Natural armor/weapons
- Innate movement (flight, swim, climb)

### 1 Flow (Basic Abilities)
- Extra movement + attack
- Defensive reaction (-2 to hit)
- Minor buff/debuff (±1-2)
- Single-target disable (save)

### 2 Flow (Tactical Abilities)
- Area effect (small burst)
- Strong buff/debuff (±2-4)
- Moderate damage boost
- Special attack mode

### 3 Flow (Powerful Abilities)
- Large area effect
- Strong disable (stun, paralyze)
- Significant damage spike
- Summon minions

### 4-5 Flow (Major Abilities)
- Devastating area attack
- Powerful buff (lasts scene)
- Multiple effects combined
- Legendary action equivalent

### 6+ Flow (Ultimate Abilities)
- Campaign-defining effects
- Massive area destruction
- Reality-warping
- Epic summons

## CONDITION QUICK REFERENCE

### Common Combat Conditions

**Prone:**
- -2 to attack rolls
- Attackers get +2 against you
- Costs movement to stand up

**Grappled:**
- Can't move away from grappler
- -2 to attack rolls
- Awareness check to escape (vs. grappler's Body)

**Stunned:**
- Can't take actions
- Attackers get +2 against you
- Lasts 1 round (usually)

**Frightened:**
- -2 to all rolls while source visible
- Can't willingly move closer to source
- Will save to end (varies)

**Slowed:**
- Half movement speed
- -2 to all actions
- Can't take reactions

**Poisoned:**
- -2 to attack rolls and Guard
- Duration varies

**Blinded:**
- -4 to attacks requiring sight
- Attackers get +2 against you

## MONSTER TACTICS BY INTELLIGENCE

### Mindless (INT -4 to -2)
Zombies, skeletons, oozes, constructs
- Follow simple orders or instincts
- No tactics or adaptation
- Fight to destruction
- Never retreat

### Animal (INT -3 to 0)
Beasts, many dire animals
- Instinctive behavior
- Pack tactics if social
- Flee when severely wounded (below 1/4 Vitality)
- Defend territory/young

### Low (INT +0 to +1)
Goblins, orcs, many humanoids
- Basic tactics
- Understand advantage/disadvantage
- Flee if losing badly
- Can be intimidated or bribed

### Average (INT +2 to +3)
Humans, most intelligent humanoids
- Good tactics
- Coordinate with allies
- Use terrain and cover
- Retreat strategically
- Negotiate if beneficial

### High (INT +4 to +6)
Dragons, liches, masterminds
- Excellent tactics
- Long-term planning
- Prepared contingencies
- Research enemies beforehand
- Strategic retreat for advantage

### Genius (INT +7+)
Ancient dragons, archliches, godlike beings
- Perfect tactical awareness
- Multi-layered plans
- Backup plans for backup plans
- Know party's capabilities and weaknesses
- Never fight without advantage

## RANGE QUICK REFERENCE

**Close:** Melee range, touch, adjacent
- Most melee weapons
- Touch spells
- Can be used while engaged in melee

**Near:** Nearby, same room, 30-60 feet
- Thrown weapons
- Ranged weapons (close range)
- Many spells
- Shouting distance

**Far:** Distant, different rooms, 60-120+ feet
- Longbows, crossbows (max range)
- Long-range spells
- Requires line of sight
- May have penalties

## QUICK CREATURE CONVERSIONS

### Making Existing Creatures Tougher

**+1 Threat Level:**
- +4-6 Vitality
- +1-2 Guard
- +2 starting Flow
- +1 to primary attribute
- Add 1 new ability

**Variant (Same Threat):**
- Swap abilities (keep similar power)
- Change damage type (fire → cold)
- Adjust attributes (Body/Mind swap)
- Reskin appearance

**Champion Template (+1 Threat):**
- +50% Vitality
- +2 to all attributes
- +2-4 starting Flow
- Add "Champion" ability (multi-attack, tactical, or powerful)

## QUICK NPC STATS BY ROLE

### Commoner (Threat 0)
Vitality 4, Guard 7, Flow 0
Primary Attribute +0 to +1
*Farmer, servant, child*

### Guard (Threat 1)
Vitality 8, Guard 10, Flow 1
Body +2, Awareness +1
Weapon: 2d6+2, deals 1d6+2
*Town watch, soldier*

### Skilled Professional (Threat 2)
Vitality 10, Guard 10-12, Flow 2-3
Primary Attribute +3, Secondary +2
Weapon/Skill: 2d6+3, appropriate effect
*Veteran, skilled mage, master craftsperson*

### Master (Threat 3)
Vitality 14-18, Guard 12-14, Flow 4-6
Primary Attribute +4 to +5
Multiple abilities or advanced skills
*Guild master, battle mage, champion*

### Legend (Threat 4)
Vitality 25+, Guard 15+, Flow 8-12
Multiple Attributes +4 to +7
Legendary abilities and resources
*Archmage, crime lord, legendary warrior*

## IMPROVISING MONSTERS AT THE TABLE

### 1. Choose Threat Level
Based on party size and difficulty desired

### 2. Use Template Stats
Pull Vitality, Guard, Flow, Attack from threat table above

### 3. Assign Attributes
- **High** (+4 to +6): Primary strength
- **Medium** (+2 to +3): Secondary abilities
- **Low** (+0 to +1): Adequate
- **Weak** (-1 to -2): Dump stats

### 4. Pick 1-2 Signature Abilities
- Threat 1: 1 simple ability
- Threat 2: 1-2 tactical abilities
- Threat 3: 2-3 abilities, one powerful
- Threat 4: 3-5 abilities, includes ultimate

### 5. Describe Tactics
One sentence: "How does it fight?"

### Example Improvisation

**Need:** Threat 2 creature for ambush

**Template Stats:**
Vitality 10, Guard 11, Flow 3
Attack +3, Damage 1d6+3

**Attributes:**
Body +3, Awareness +3, others +0/-1

**Abilities:**
1. **Ambush** (Passive): +2 to first attack from hiding
2. **Pounce** (1 Flow): Move and attack, knock prone on 10+

**Tactics:** Ambush from trees, pounce on isolated targets, flee if hurt

**Result:** Forest Panther - done in 2 minutes

## MONSTER TYPE TRAITS

### Beast
- Natural animals
- Instinctive behavior
- No magic or tools

### Humanoid
- Intelligent bipeds
- Use tools and tactics
- Can negotiate

### Undead
- Immune: poison, disease, exhaustion
- Often mindless
- May have life drain

### Construct
- Immune: poison, psychic, mind-affecting
- Follows orders literally
- Unusual vulnerabilities

### Dragon
- High attributes across board
- Breath weapon
- Dragon fear
- Legendary resistance

### Aberration
- Weird abilities
- Alien psychology
- Break normal rules

### Fiend (Demon/Devil)
- Damage resistance (non-magical)
- Vulnerable to radiant
- May reform after death

## LAIR ACTION EXAMPLES

For legendary adversaries in prepared environments:

**Initiative Count 20 (lose ties), choose one:**

### Physical
- Tremor: All creatures save or fall prone
- Environmental hazard: Lava, spikes, poison gas (2d6 damage, save for half)
- Difficult terrain: Zone becomes hard to move through

### Magical
- Arcane surge: Next spell costs -2 Flow
- Dispel: One magical effect ends
- Telekinetic attack: Throw object at target (2d6 damage)

### Summons
- Minions appear: 1d4 Threat 1 creatures
- Environmental creatures: Animate objects, awaken plants
- Call reinforcements: Existing allies arrive

### Tactical
- Visibility: Darkness, fog, bright light
- Movement: Create/remove cover, walls, obstacles
- Information: Scrying, detect location, sense magic

**Frequency:** Legendary adversaries typically act once per round on initiative 20

When creature forces save:

10 + relevant attribute

12 + relevant attribute

**Hard DC:**
15 + relevant attribute

### Common Save Types

**Body:** Poison, physical force, exhaustion

**Awareness:** Dodge area effects, notice hidden, react quickly
**Will:** Resist mind control, fear, possession
**Presence:** Social pressure, intimidation, deception

## SOCIAL ENCOUNTER QUICK RULES

### NPC Reaction Roll (2d6 + Presence)

**2-6:** Hostile (attack, refuse, oppose)
**7-9:** Unfriendly (suspicious, unhelpful)
**10-11:** Neutral (indifferent, transactional)
**12-14:** Friendly (helpful, willing to assist)
**15+:** Allied (enthusiastic, go out of way)

**Modifiers:**
- Party helped NPC/faction before: +2
- Party harmed NPC/faction before: -2
- Appropriate bribe/gift: +1 to +3
- Threatening NPC: -2 to -4
- Sympathetic request: +1 to +2

## TREASURE BY THREAT LEVEL

### Minions (Threat 1)
- **Individual:** 1-10 gold, basic equipment
- **Group (5-6):** 20-50 gold total, maybe 1 minor magic item

### Standard (Threat 2)
- **Individual:** 25-100 gold, quality equipment
- **Group (3-4):** 100-300 gold total, 1-2 minor magic items

### Champion (Threat 3)
- **Individual:** 200-500 gold, masterwork equipment, 1 magic item
- **Lair:** 500-2000 gold, 2-4 magic items, valuables

### Legendary (Threat 4)
- **Lair:** 2000-10,000+ gold, 4-8 magic items, artifacts, treasures
- **Story rewards:** Territory, titles, followers, unique items

*Adjust based on campaign wealth level and narrative appropriateness*

## QUICK MONSTER CUSTOMIZATION

### Damage Type Variants

**Swap damage type, add flavor:**
- Fire → Cold (ice breath)
- Slashing → Bludgeoning (club vs sword)
- Physical → Energy (force, lightning, etc.)

### Simple Templates

**Undead Template:**
- Immune: poison, disease, mind-affecting
- Reduce Presence by -2
- Add "Undead Resilience" (hard to kill)

**Elemental Template:**
- Immune: corresponding element
- Vulnerable: opposing element
- Add elemental damage to attacks

**Fiendish Template:**
- Resist: non-magical damage
- Vulnerable: radiant damage
- Add fire or necrotic to attacks

**Celestial Template:**
- Resist: radiant, necrotic
- Add radiant damage to attacks
- Aura: heal allies 1 vitality/round (close range)

## RUNNING LARGE COMBATS

### Grouping Minions

**Same Type in Group:**
- Make single attack roll for all
- Each that hits deals separate damage
- Track Vitality as group total
- Remove individuals as damage accumulates

**Example:**
4 goblins attack fighter
- Roll 2d6+2 once
- Result: 10 (hit)
- Roll 4d6 damage (one for each goblin)

### Splitting Attention

**Multiple Groups:**
- Group by type and location
- Initiative: Each group acts on same count
- Focus: Each group picks different target

### Quick Clearing

**Weak Enemies:**
- Any attack that drops below 0 kills instantly (no death saves)
- Clearing minions quickly keeps combat moving

## WHEN THINGS GO WRONG

### Party Too Weak

**Mid-Combat Adjustments:**
- Reduce enemy Vitality (secretly)
- Have enemies flee when "winning" (tactical retreat)
- Reinforcements arrive for party
- Morale break: Enemies surrender or flee

### Party Too Strong

**Mid-Combat Adjustments:**
- Reinforcements for enemies
- Enemy uses environment (cover, terrain)
- Tactical improvement (enemies smarter)
- Higher threat enemy arrives

### Combat Dragging

**Speed It Up:**
- Group enemy turns
- Encourage decisive actions
- Timer for player turns (30-60 sec)
- Skip to narrative conclusion if outcome clear

## MEMORABLE MONSTER MOMENTS

### Give Them Personality

**Even in Combat:**
- Distinctive voice/sounds
- Unique fighting style
- Reactions to damage ("HOW DARE YOU!")
- Intelligent enemies talk/taunt

### Make Them Memorable

**Visual Distinctiveness:**
- Unique appearance detail
- Signature move or ability
- Dramatic entrance
- Interesting environment

### Create Stories

**Beyond Stats:**
- Why is it here?
- What does it want?
- How does it react to PCs?
- What happens if it escapes?
