## MOMENTUM REFERENCE

### Players Gain Momentum

- **Defeating any adversary:** +1 Momentum
- **Defeating major villain:** +2 Momentum (GM discretion)
- **Successful attack in Aggressive Stance:** +1 Momentum

### Adversaries Gain Momentum

- Same rules as players (if intelligent)
- Mindless creatures don't track Momentum
- Most minor opponents don't manage Momentum tactically

## POWERFUL ADVERSARY RULES

### Multiple Actions

Major adversaries may act more than once per round.

**Critical rule:** Limit to 2 ATTACKS per round maximum

Third action options: Movement, abilities, defensive stance, environment interaction

### Legendary Resistance

Major villains can shrug off 2-3 effects per fight

Use sparingly - players should feel their abilities matter

### Lair Advantages

In their territory, powerful adversaries may have:

- Environmental hazards they're immune to
- Arriving reinforcements
- Magical effects woven into the location

## BEHAVIOR BY INTELLIGENCE

### Mindless (zombies, constructs, oozes)

- Attack nearest target
- No tactics, no retreat
- Fight until destroyed
- Predictable patterns

### Animal (wolves, bears, giant spiders)

- Pack tactics if social
- Target weak/wounded
- Flee when badly hurt
- Defend territory

### Average (bandits, soldiers, cultists)

- Coordinate with allies
- Use cover and terrain
- Retreat strategically
- Can be negotiated with

### High (dragons, archmages, masterminds)

- Complex tactics
- Prepared contingencies
- Exploit weaknesses
- Strategic retreats

## IMPROVISING ADVERSARIES

### 30-Second Creation

1. **Choose capability level** (inexperienced/trained/veteran/legendary)
2. **Assign Guard and Vitality** from table above
3. **Set combat modifier** (+1 to +5)
4. **Pick one ability** that fits their concept
5. **Decide their personality** (aggressive, cautious, protective, cowardly)

### Example

**Need:** A corrupt merchant's bodyguard

**Statistics:** Trained level - Guard 16, Vitality 18, Combat +3

**Ability:** "Protective Stance" - When the merchant is attacked, can intercept (1 Momentum)

**Personality:** Professional, loyal to whoever's paying, will surrender if merchant flees

## ENCOUNTER BUILDING GUIDELINES

### Action Distribution

**The Golden Rule:** Each side should have roughly equal total actions per round.

| Party Size | Recommended Enemy Actions |
| ---------- | ------------------------- |
| 3 PCs      | 3-4 enemy actions total   |
| 4 PCs      | 4-5 enemy actions total   |
| 5 PCs      | 5-6 enemy actions total   |

**WARNING:** A party of 4 vs 8 enemies (8 actions) will be overwhelmed regardless of individual enemy weakness!

### Balancing Many Weak Enemies

When using multiple enemies:

- **4+ enemies:** Reduce their damage by -1 each, OR give them half normal Vitality
- **6+ enemies:** Consider having some flee after allies fall
- **Use minions:** 1 Vitality enemies that die in one hit but still threaten with numbers

### Difficulty Tiers

| Difficulty  | Enemy Actions | Enemy Power Level | Expected Outcome       |
| ----------- | ------------- | ----------------- | ---------------------- |
| Easy        | Party -1      | Inexperienced     | Win with minor losses  |
| Standard    | Party +0      | Trained           | Win with resource cost |
| Challenging | Party +1      | Veteran           | Win with major effort  |
| Deadly      | Party +2      | Legendary         | Possible defeat        |

### Party Rank vs Enemy Capability

| Party Rank | Appropriate Major Adversary | Appropriate Group      |
| ---------- | --------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Novice     | Trained (1)           | 3-4 Inexperienced      |
| Seasoned   | Veteran (1)           | 4-5 Trained            |
| Veteran    | Legendary (1)         | 2 Veterans + 3 Trained |
| Heroic     | Legendary + minions   | 3-4 Veterans           |

### Making Major Adversary Fights Work

Solo major adversaries need help to avoid being overwhelmed:

- **Multi-action:** Major adversaries act 2-3 times per round (max 2 attacks)
- **Minions:** Add 2-4 weak allies to split party attention
- **Lair actions:** Environment acts once per round
- **Phases:** Adversary transforms or gains new abilities at 50% Vitality

### Quick Encounter Math

**Average PC damage per round:** 7-10 (weapon + attribute)
**Average PC Guard:** 15-17 (base 12 + attribute + gear)

## WHEN THINGS GO WRONG

### Fight Too Easy

- Reinforcements arrive
- Environmental complication
- Adversary reveals hidden ability

### Fight Too Hard

- Adversaries make tactical errors
- Morale breaks, some flee
- Environmental opportunity for players

### Combat Dragging

- Adversaries make decisive moves
- Narrate conclusion if outcome obvious
- "With their leader down, the rest surrender"
